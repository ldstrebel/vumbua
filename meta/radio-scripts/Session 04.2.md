# Session 4.2 — Pre-Race Broadcast: The First Circuit-Run

**Host:** Valerius "Val" Sterling
**Guest:** Professor Kante
**When:** Morning of the Circuit-Run, before the first horn

---

*[Transatlantic accent — smooth, rolling, barely-contained. He is looking down through the transparent floor-panes of a Gilded Zephyr airship. The basin below is alive.]*

*[wide, genuine wonder]*
"Citizens of Vumbua — good morning, or what passes for morning when you are hovering one hundred and fifty feet above the most extraordinary arena on the floating world. I am Valerius Sterling. I am exactly where I promised I would be. And below me — through the most remarkable floor-panes that money and the goodwill of House Gilded can arrange — the Apex Ring is *alive*."

*[deliberate, awed]*
"If you have not yet seen the new terrain layout — and I know some of you came straight from the exam halls, bless you for your dedication — find your seats, get your eyes down into that basin. This is the first time the arena configuration has been updated in a generation. The Mizizi Petrified Forest is in there. Those of you from the forests will recognise it. The column-trunks are respectful — whoever built the model has done their homework. There is water now. A cut channel running from the western forest through to the central island where the Grand Resonator stands. It moves. It breathes. And in the southwest corner: the Ash-Blood Isles. Volcanic terrain. Simulated ridgelines. The scree and the heat shimmer of the Isles themselves."

*[pause, sincere]*
"It is the first time that geography has appeared in this arena. The first time. And before the horns, I want to take a moment to tell you about the woman who was supposed to be very much a part of what you were going to see today."

*[warm, building]*
"Lady Ignis Scorch. Ash-Blood Matriarch. Harmony's most significant voting bloc. And — as it turns out — an exceptional designer. She has spent the better part of the last year doing two things simultaneously: a political tour of every integrated node territory in Harmony — meeting each representative in person, learning the terrain of governance before casting a single vote — and the design of the Ash-Blood origin spire for this race."

"Handcrafted from leidian itself. The genuine heat-stone. Five columns, each one veined with magma visible through hairline cracks in the surface. On sync, the design called for no flash, no bang. A pulse. Low. Resonant. The sound of a forge being lit. Something that would say to every spectator in these stands: *this is what the Ash-Bloods contribute*."

*[a real pause — not performed]*
"Word reached the studio this morning that the spire would not be making its debut today."

*[dry, decisive]*
"I was not prepared to let that pass without understanding why. So I called in a favor."

*[announcing]*
"Citizens — joining me now, graciously agreeing to climb to a height he described as, and I am quoting directly: '*unreasonably vertical*' — the Academy's Professor of Harmonics, the foremost expert on ether resonance and node measurement in the modern era. Professor Kante — welcome to the Zephyrs."

---

**Professor Kante:** *[slow, deliberate, Indian accent — every word chosen — a faint discomfort at altitude that he is managing with great dignity]* "Thank you, Valerius. The floor here is... very transparent."

**Valerius:** *[smiling through it]* "The floor is the point, Professor. Now — straight to it. Lady Ignis's spire. Why is it not in the race today?"

**Professor Kante:** *[pause — the patience of someone who has chosen the right word a thousand times]* "There were... some issues. In the final calculation of the node's value. The resonance data for the Ash-Blood connection has not yet settled to a number we can model with confidence. Until it does, we cannot accurately represent what that node produces. And in the Circuit-Run, accuracy in a node is not... optional."

**Valerius:** *[pressing, but not hostile]* "Has this ever happened before? A node being held back from the race?"

**Professor Kante:** *[thoughtful — this is the question he has been waiting for]* "For three hundred years, we have had only minor nodes. Small adjustments. And the calibration has been... manageable. But reach back further. Before the Plateau. Before the Night of Sparks — four hundred years ago, when a city block's worth of resonators burned because someone was in a hurry to push a number they were not certain of into a live loop. The data was close. *Close* was not *right*. And there is — what do you like, know — no such thing as close, in a closed resonance loop. Not for the spires. Not for the resonators. Not for the rigs that will be hitting those nodes this morning."

**Valerius:** *[quiet for a beat]* "That's a long shadow to cast over a race-day morning, Professor."

**Professor Kante:** *[simply, firmly]* "It is the correct shadow. I want to be clear about this, Valerius. The engineers — myself included — wanted this spire in the race. There is a room full of people who worked on this alongside Lady Ignis, who believe in the design, who *want* it to be seen. We are not indifferent to the delay. But these things must be done right. Not because we doubt the Ash-Bloods. Because getting it wrong would be considerably worse than waiting."

**Valerius:** *[genuine respect — then the professional pivot]* "Professor Kante. As always, clarity disguised as caution. Thank you for coming up."

**Professor Kante:** *[already moving toward the stairs]* "I intend to take the stairs."

**Valerius:** *[to the audience, fond]* "He's taking the stairs."

---

*[Valerius — alone again, looking down through the floor. The basin is filling. The crowd noise is coming up from below.]*

*[practical, warm]*
"Right. Now — for those of you still sorting out where to put yourselves for the next few hours, let me save you some time."

"If you are at the **Bleacher-Walls**: get a Scout-Spotter from the Tier 3 vendors before the first horn. Brass binoculars, Loom-calibrated for node tracking, twelve copper for the race. This is not a suggestion — the basin is a mile across and the spires do not wait for the naked eye. You will want them."

"If you are on the **Rim-Tracker**: find the east-facing car early. The Tracker runs the full perimeter but the east shelf is where late-race positioning tends to resolve. Put yourself there before Horn 2 and you will not regret it."

"And if you are lucky enough to be in one of the **Zephyrs** — you already know. Enjoy your floor. It holds."

*[building into prediction]*
"As for the race itself — my prediction, for whatever a Sterling's prediction is worth before the horns have sounded. The field is going to sprint southwest. The Petrified Forest is new terrain. Nobody has raced it. New terrain draws competitors the way light draws moths — they want to know what spawns in there first. Your best early view is **south and southwest**, where the column-trunks will funnel the opening sprint into something worth watching."

"After that: get your eyes east. The Shelf has reliable mid-race node real estate, the Tracker runs right alongside it, and anything that survives the forest sprint will be making decisions there."

*[measured, deliberate — a very Sterling-shaped tease]*
"Watch the north. I will say nothing more about the north. But watch it."

*[signing off — the voice of someone who has been doing this their whole life and still loves it]*
"I am Valerius Sterling. One hundred and fifty feet above the Apex Ring, in an airship with a transparent floor. Twelve teams. Three horns. One Grand Resonator."

"Momentum is Life — and today, citizens, it has nowhere to go but *down*."

"The horns will tell you when."

---
*[end of broadcast]*
