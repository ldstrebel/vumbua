## Part 1: Morning Routines

The sun rises on the first official day of classes at Vumbua Academy. In the cafeteria courtyard, **[[Iggy]]** is experiencing a culinary awakening. Sitting with **[[Lomi]]** and **[[Ignatius]]**, the Trench-Kin is surrounded by an enormous pile of diverse food items, methodically taking a single perfect bite out of each muffin to decide which is best.

Lomi is stressed. The reality of the Academy's rigor is setting in, and he reminds the party that they have a massive test at the end of the week. "The muffins... they're a little carb-heavy for me in the mornings," Lomi says, waving off Iggy's offerings. "I get a crash. We got a big test. We gotta be on our game this week."

Ignatius tries to calm him down, confident that they can just track down **[[Lucky]]** later to get the study guide **[[Valerius Sterling|Val]]** wrote. Lomi agrees but insists they stay focused for the day.

Meanwhile, elsewhere on campus, **[[Aggie]]** and **[[Britt]]** begin their own morning routines, preparing for the grueling week ahead.

## Part 2: Logistics and Chalk

The party heads to their first class: Aetheric Ballistics & Logistics. The professor is a no-nonsense instructor who skips introductions and jumps straight into complex kinematic equations for vessel transport.

Ignatius, still wearing his bulky armor and possessing a dismal Evasion score of 7, is struggling to keep up. Without warning, the professor hurls a piece of chalk directly at Ignatius. It arcs perfectly through the air, completely ignoring the wind in the room due to precise calculations, and smacks Ignatius right in the forehead with a dull *crack*.

"Hit him in the forehead because he has such a low evasion," the GM notes. "It doesn't do any damage... but it might do a little emotional damage."

Ignatius immediately starts taking notes.

As the dense, hyper-accelerated lecture wraps up, Lomi realizes he missed the first section. He leans over to the well-dressed student sitting next to him—**[[Finch Gable]]**.

**LOMI:** Hey, I missed that first section that he was talking about. Do you mind if I take a peek at your notes real quick? I'll just quick copy it down.  
**FINCH GABLE:** Yes, I do mind. I am unfortunately unable to share this.  
**LOMI:** No, like physically unable to? 'Cause you could just tilt your notebook for a second—  
**FINCH GABLE:** I think you still have chalk dust from your friend's forehead, and I don't want the deep part of that. Best of luck. I will sit in a different row tomorrow.  

Lomi aggressively bumps the snobby student with his backpack on the way out. "Have a good day."

## Part 3: The Circuit Race

On the way out of the lecture hall, the students stop to read an announcement posted on the bulletin board. It outlines the grueling schedule:
- Two classes a day for three days (six classes total).
- Thursday morning: One class.
- Thursday afternoon: Extracurricular "Circuit Race at Apex Ring."
- Friday: The Final Exam, followed by Team Assignments from the Loom. Over 20,000 students will be eliminated.

Ignatius proposes they ditch classes to find a ship equipped with the "logistical equipment" (cannons) to get some hands-on practice. Lomi is hesitant to skip, but absolutely lights up when the Circuit Race is mentioned.

Lomi eagerly explains the sport to the others. The Circuit Race (colloquially called the "Reszo Race") is Harmony's premier sport. Teams pilot various custom vehicles—from sailboats to massive mechs—around a mile-wide ring.

The goal is to navigate the arena, strike erupting "Energy Spires" with a metal rod to collect designated energy, and use that energy to light the central node. Each city designs its own distinct spire. 

"I've been going ever since I was a kid," Lomi says. "My dad got seats high up... but I'd have to work quasi-underground at first and make sure the spires were powering correctly. It is a sight to behold." Lomi suggests they pool their money to place bets, or even consider racing themselves if they can build a vehicle.

## Part 4: Trading Secrets with Lucky

That evening, Lomi, Ignatius, and Iggy spend two hours discreetly rolling through the campus until they find **[[Lucky]]**. He's set up against a building near the Block 99 bonfire, standing on a crate with a tarp ready to scoop his inventory at a moment’s notice. Lomi notices the elusive **[[Sarre]]** lingering quietly near Lucky.

"Oi, I know these guys," Lucky says, jumping down. "You want to get lucky? I bet I know what you need."

Lomi asks for the study guide Val mentioned. Lucky grins, revealing Val's rule: he can't accept money for it. The price is secrets and stories.

Lomi starts by recounting the party's game of Crown and Ruin at the Celestial Lounge, embellishing the story of getting Val drunk and passing out. Lomi even produces the *actual* golden celestial die he palmed from Val as proof. Lucky is impressed but demands more to hand over the notes. 

Ignatius steps up, weaving a magnificent, millennia-old creation myth:

**IGNATIUS:** Let's go back millennia. At that time, there was a Great Dragon of the Sky and a Great Dragon of the Sea. They respected each other but were separated by the horizon... They longed to meet. A trickster demon promised the Sky Dragon they could meet if he climbed as high as he could and dove down.
The Sky Dragon rose to the sun, turned, and crashed back toward the sea. He could not slow down. He started to burn, burning all the way to the ocean.
The Sea Dragon, a kind dragon, opened its arms and caught the Sky Dragon, extinguishing the flames. The Sky Dragon burned up but formed our islands... It is this loving embrace that heats our islands and provides us with the stones that heat our stuff today.

Lucky is completely floored by the lore. "Damn man, I thought it was going to be a joke... That's pretty cool shit."

Lucky hands over the 10-question study guide. However, he refuses to hand over the actual *notes and answers* for the test unless they provide him with *real* secrets. He zeroes in on Iggy, sensing the Trench-Kin is hiding something massive.

Iggy awkwardly deflects by pulling out his crude inventions: a "viewfinder" (team glass), a "goggle straw", and a flashlight. Lucky is baffled but intrigued when Iggy accidentally produces the strange gold receipt from the entrance exam.

Seeing the receipt, Lucky's demeanor shifts. "You don't know what that means? Let's get out of the main road. Why don't we go have a chat..."

The session ends on a sudden cliffhanger as Lucky ushers Lomi, Ignatius, and Iggy into a private "interrogation chamber" side room to discuss the true cost of the test answers.
