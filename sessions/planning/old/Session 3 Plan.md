### üé≤ Game 1: "Crown & Ruin" *(Push-Your-Luck Dice Game)*

**What it teaches:** The Hope/Fear dice, Stress accumulation, Hope/Fear token economy.

**The Drink:** A tray of "Void Shots" ‚Äî a glowing, bitter Academy drink that burns going down. **Every time you mark a Stress in this game, you take a shot in-fiction.** Val pours with a grin.

#### Rules

1. Players take turns rolling the **Hope die and Fear die** (the actual 2d12).
2. **If Hope ‚â• Fear:** You bank the difference as points. Earn a **Hope token** (real ‚Äî you keep this).
3. **If Fear > Hope:** You **take a shot and mark a Stress.** The GM earns a **Fear token.**
4. **If they match (doubles):** *"Crown & Ruin!"* ‚Äî Choose: **double your banked points** OR **lose them all.** (Teaches risk assessment.)
5. After each roll, you can **stop and bank your points** or **push for another roll.**
6. **First to 20 points wins.**
7. **If you hit 3 Stress, you're bust** ‚Äî you're out of the round, slumped over the table.

#### GM Notes

- Keep it **fast** ‚Äî 2-3 rounds max. This is a warm-up, not the main event.
- The Hope/Fear tokens they earn are **real and persist** ‚Äî they carry into the ambush scene. This makes the game feel consequential.
- If a PC busts out at 3 Stress, that Stress carries forward too. The shots were real. The hangover is real.

#### How the NPCs Play

**Valentine ‚Äî The Showman**
- **Never banks early.** He'll push to the edge and grin. *"Banking already? How very... cautious of you."*
- **Light-hearted trash talk.** He's not mean ‚Äî he's *entertaining.* He narrates his rolls like a radio commentator: *"And Sterling rolls again! The crowd goes wild! The dice‚Äîoh. Oh no."*
- **Takes his shots with style.** When he marks Stress, he raises the glass, toasts the table, and drinks with a wince he immediately masks. *"Perfectly fine. That one tasted like victory. Delayed victory."*
- **Strategically clever.** He watches other players' risk tolerance and adjusts his commentary to pressure cautious players into pushing: *"You know, my father always said ‚Äî Fortune doesn't wait for the timid."*
- **If he busts out:** He takes it with exaggerated grace: *"I believe... the room is tilting. That means I've won at something."*

**Ember ‚Äî The Overachiever**
- **Ultra-aggressive.** She pushes *every single time.* She doesn't understand the concept of banking ‚Äî it feels like cowardice to her.
- **Treats it like an exam.** She's not playing for fun ‚Äî she's playing to *prove* she belongs at this table, at this party, in Harmony. Every win is validation.
- **Takes her shots like medicine.** Slams them back without flinching, then immediately asks whose turn it is. No time for sentiment.
- **Observant and calculating.** Despite her aggression, she's watching everyone's tells carefully. She notices who hesitates, who flinches at the dice, who looks at their banked points.
- **Trying too hard to fit in.** She laughs a beat too late at Val's jokes. She uses Harmony slang slightly wrong. She's desperate to be seen as *one of them,* not "the Ash-Blood girl."
- **If she busts out:** Her composure cracks. A flash of genuine frustration ‚Äî she wanted to win *so badly.* She covers it quickly: *"Again. Let's go again."*

### üÉè Game 2: "Truths & Treachery" *(Truth-or-Lie Social Game)*

**What it teaches:** Contested rolls, how different stats work against each other, retcon narration.

**The Drink:** Same Void Shots. **You take a shot (mark Stress) if you guess wrong.**

#### Rules

1. One player is the **Teller.** They tell the table something about themselves, their clan, their past ‚Äî **either completely true or a total lie.**
2. The Teller makes a **Presence roll** (stat + 2d12) ‚Äî this is their "poker face" score. *They do NOT reveal the number yet.*
3. Every other player makes an **Instinct roll** (stat + 2d12) ‚Äî this is their "read" on the Teller.
4. **The Teller reveals the truth:** Was it real or a lie?
5. **Compare rolls after the reveal:**
   - If your Instinct roll **‚â• the Teller's Presence roll**, you guessed correctly. Earn a **Hope token.**
   - If your Instinct roll **< the Teller's Presence roll**, you guessed wrong. **Take a shot. Mark Stress.**
6. **Retcon the guesses:** After the reveal, players narrate what their character's reaction was *in the moment*. The GM asks: *"So [PC], when they said that ‚Äî what did you think? Did you buy it?"* Players whose rolls succeeded get to say they saw through it; players who failed get to describe being fooled.

#### Why the Retcon Works

- The GM doesn't need to manage a guessing phase ‚Äî just let everyone roll, reveal, then narrate retroactively.
- This is how Daggerheart handles many social situations: roll first, narrate the fiction to match.
- It lets players practice **collaborative storytelling** ‚Äî they build the scene after the dice tell them the outcome.
- **Bonus:** Players learn things about each other's backstories through the truths and lies. This is *actual character development* disguised as a drinking game.

#### GM Notes

- Val and Ember go first to demonstrate the format. Then PCs take turns.
- Encourage players to use **real backstory** for their truths ‚Äî this is free character-building.
- NPC statements are pre-written below ‚Äî pick 2-3 for each NPC depending on time.
- **Ember is taking mental notes for Valerius's radio show.** Anything juicy the PCs reveal may appear on air later.

#### How the NPCs Play

**Valentine ‚Äî The Smooth Operator**
- Val treats T&T like a performance. He leans back, steeples his fingers, and delivers each statement like a theatre monologue.
- **High Presence.** He's *genuinely good at lying* ‚Äî smooth voice, steady eye contact, confident rhythm. His poker face is championship-level.
- **Strategically vulnerable.** He mixes real emotional truths with outrageous lies, making it hard to tell which is which. He *wants* people to underestimate his sincerity.
- When he reads others, he does it with a raised eyebrow and a knowing smile: *"Interesting. Very interesting."*
- **If caught in a lie:** Takes the shot gracefully. *"Well played. I almost had you."*
- **If his truth is doubted:** Genuine flash of hurt he quickly masks. A crack in the armour.

**Ember ‚Äî The Intensity**
- Ember treats T&T like an interrogation. She leans forward, studies faces, barely blinks.
- **Low Presence, high Instinct.** She's bad at lying (too intense, too earnest) but *excellent* at reading others. She stares right through people.
- When someone tells their statement, Ember is visibly analysing ‚Äî head tilted, eyes narrowed. She looks like she's trying to decode them.
- **Trying to fit in.** She wants to be fun and casual about it, but she can't help being competitive. She'll say something like *"This is such a fun game!"* with the energy of someone filing a police report.
- **If she guesses wrong:** Takes the shot fast, then immediately explains her reasoning to nobody: *"No, but see, the way you said it ‚Äî the cadence was‚Äî"*
- **When it's her turn to tell:** She gets nervous. These truths cut close to home. Her voice drops. She stops performing.

## Phase 2: The Ambush

### Setting

**Location:** A dark stretch of campus between the Celestial Lounge and the dorms ‚Äî perhaps an unlit courtyard or maintenance corridor. Late at night, few witnesses.

**Time:** After the PCs leave the Celestial Lounge. The campus is quiet, most students already in bed before exams.

**Mood:** Sudden shift from warm social energy to cold, tense danger. Shadows, silence, then violence.

---

### The Situation

The PCs are ambushed by **Tus** and **Zyykl**, two burly young men from [[Nstyl]] ‚Äî the Silent Silt city where sound is physically impossible and everyone communicates through sign language. They don't speak; they *can't* speak. They just attack.

But Tus and Zyykl aren't the real threat ‚Äî they've been **manipulated** by **Azor**, a street-smart schemer from [[Settika]] who used [[Prism Falls]] water to convince them this was their only shot at redemption.

---

### Azor's Scheme

**The Setup:** Tus and Zyykl both **failed out of the first round of entrance exams.** They're devastated ‚Äî they came all the way from [[Nstyl]] to prove themselves, and they believed in the Academy more than anything.

**The Con:** Azor tracked them down and used water from [[Prism Falls]] (Settika's node resource ‚Äî water that reveals the true nature of whatever it touches). He told them:

> *"I know what you really are. The water showed me. You're Exploranauts ‚Äî real ones. The test was wrong. All you need is a receipt from a current student, and you can retake the exam. The Academy will see they failed the wrong people."*

Tus and Zyykl **believe in the power of the Prism Falls**. Back in [[Nstyl]], Settika's water is respected, almost sacred. If the water says they're Exploranauts, they must be. They're naive, earnest, and desperate.

**The Truth:** Azor knows this won't work. The [[The Loom|Loom]] receipts aren't transferable IDs ‚Äî they're point-in-time documents. The Loom recognises students based on how they interact with it, not by a piece of paper. Azor doesn't care about helping Tus and Zyykl. He's a mugger, plain and simple.

**The Real Plan:** Azor figured that the only students dumb enough to be out this late ‚Äî the night before the most important week of most people's lives ‚Äî would be the **rich and drunk ones.** Prudent students would be asleep. So he waits in the shadows, lets Tus and Zyykl do the dangerous work, and plans to take whatever valuables fall out of pockets during the scuffle.

### Key NPCs

#### Azor ‚Äî The Man from Settika

A world-wise, street-smart manipulator from [[Settika]]. He knows how things work ‚Äî Loom receipts, Academy bureaucracy, Prism Falls water. He uses that knowledge to exploit people who don't.

**Key traits:**
- **Sneaky and manipulative** ‚Äî operates through proxies, never gets his hands dirty.
- **Not from a clan** ‚Äî he's a city kid from [[Settika]], where the Prism Falls resource is just another tool.
- **Knows the con doesn't work** ‚Äî he told Tus and Zyykl what they wanted to hear. The receipts are worthless.
- **Actual goal:** Mug rich students and disappear before morning.

**During the fight:**
- Azor is **watching from the shadows**, not engaging directly. He lets Tus and Zyykl take the risk.
- If the fight goes badly (PCs are too strong), he may slip away entirely ‚Äî a loose thread for later.
- If the PCs are losing, he may step in to grab a crystal battery or coin purse.

#### Tus & Zyykl ‚Äî The Nstyl Brothers

Two large, powerful young men from [[Nstyl]], the city with [[harmony-nodes#13 Nstyl|Silent Silt]] ‚Äî where all sound is absorbed and everyone communicates through sign language. They will not speak during the encounter because they physically cannot ‚Äî spoken language doesn't exist in their world.

**Key traits:**
- **Sign language only** ‚Äî the PCs can't reason with them verbally. A PC who knows sign language could potentially communicate.
- **Names are gestures, not words** ‚Äî "Tus" and "Zyykl" are outsider approximations of their hand-sign names. Different people pronounce them differently. (See [[harmony-nodes#13 Nstyl|Nstyl naming conventions]].)
- **Naive, not evil** ‚Äî they genuinely believe they're supposed to be Academy students. Azor used Prism Falls water to convince them. They think they're *reclaiming* something that was wrongly taken.
- **Failed the entrance exam** ‚Äî they're heartbroken about it. This isn't rage; it's desperation.
- **Burly and strong** ‚Äî these are big boys. Farm-strong from the Nstyl quarries. Built for labour, not subtlety.
- **If defeated:** They seem confused and ashamed. Once the adrenaline fades, they realise something is wrong. If a PC can communicate in sign language, Tus will try to explain ‚Äî *"The water said we were real. The man said the paper would prove it."*
