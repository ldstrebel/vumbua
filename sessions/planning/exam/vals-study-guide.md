## VOLUMETRIC CARTOGRAPHY

**1. The Z-Axis Paradox**
A floating island rotates at 45° per minute. A Solar Gale pushes against rotation. If you must pass through the gale, what is the safest maneuver?
- [ ] A) The Synchronized Helix
- [ ] B) Drop Anchor and Wait
- [x] C) The "Needle-Thread" Intercept
- [ ] D) Dead-Stick Drop
> **Val's Note:** C. It's always C. The professors love a dramatic maneuver. Tell them you'll "thread the needle" and they'll eat it up. Also, islands don't rotate that fast. Who wrote this?

**2. Aether-Graph Plotting**
When mapping a non-Euclidean archipelago, what tool must be recalibrated every hour?
- [x] A) Brass Sextant
- [ ] B) Resonance Tuning Fork
- [ ] C) Silver-lined Cartography Graph
- [ ] D) Umber Crystal Weights
> **Val's Note:** TRICK QUESTION. They never ask about the tools, they ask about the procedure. Say you'd recalibrate the Sextant FIRST, then check the Silver-lined Graph. They've used this trap since I was a first-year.

## HARMONIC RESONANCE

**3. The Feral Resonance**
A Node hums in B-Minor but is weeping spores. What is the standard protocol?
- [x] A) Sympathetic Dissonance
- [ ] B) Standard Overwrite
- [ ] C) Passive Observation
- [ ] D) Frequency Clamp
> **Val's Note:** A. If it's weeping, it's sad. "Sympathetic" is literally in the name. They want to see you have basic empathy for rocks.

**4. The Core Crack**
If a Node's structural casing is fractured, where should you direct stabilizing energy?
- [ ] A) The Internal Core
- [ ] B) The Geometric Center
- [x] C) The Crack Itself
- [ ] D) Rapid Burst
> **Val's Note:** C. The Crack Itself. It's like putting glue in a broken vase. Anyone who says 'Internal Core' wants to blow up the ship.

## REALITY ANCHOR DRILLS

**5. The Anchor of Self**
When Ether-Mist threatens your ego, what is the strongest conceptual anchor?
- [ ] A) The Law (Recite the 7 Laws of Harmony)
- [ ] B) The Future (Visualize graduation)
- [ ] C) The Void (Let it take you)
- [x] D) The Flaw (Focus on failure or pain)
> **Val's Note:** D. The Flaw. Happy thoughts fade when you're terrified, but remembering that time you embarrassed yourself in front of the whole class? That sticks with you forever. Solid anchor.

**6. Spatial Distortion**
Gravity shifts 90 degrees to starboard abruptly. What is the immediate survival protocol?
- [x] A) Magnetic Boots
- [ ] B) External Bracing (Grab a railing)
- [ ] C) Adjust Internal Mass Density
- [ ] D) Harmony Breathing
> **Val's Note:** A. Magnetic boots. They're going to try and get you to say "Deep Breathing". Don't fall for the hippie pseudo-science. Gravity doesn't care about your breath.

## XENOBIOLOGICAL SCAVENGING

**7. First Contact Protocol**
A terra-tribesman approaches with a drawn weapon. The absolute first step is:
- [ ] A) Vulnerability (Lay down weapons)
- [ ] B) De-escalation (Offer trade)
- [x] C) Authority (Project strength)
- [ ] D) Superior Firepower (Disarm)
> **Val's Note:** C. Project Strength. If you lay your weapons down in the frontier, you're just a highly educated buffet. Show them you mean business, then talk.

**8. The Toxic Bloom**
You find a rare, highly valuable flower, but touching its petals causes paralysis. How do you harvest it?
- [x] A) Partial Harvest with Lead Gloves
- [ ] B) Burn on Sight
- [ ] C) Environmental Masking
- [ ] D) Priority Extraction with Tongs
> **Val's Note:** A. Lead Gloves. Tongs crush the petals, ruining the value. I know this from personal... a friend told me.

## CRISIS MANAGEMENT & HISTORY

**9. The Sacrifice**
You have one hull patch left. The Kitchen and the Engine Room both breach simultaneously. What is the correct priority?
- [x] A) The Engine Gamble (Patch engine)
- [ ] B) Command Compliance (Patch kitchen)
- [ ] C) The Split (Attempt both)
- [ ] D) Wait for Orders
> **Val's Note:** A. Patch the Engine. If the engine dies, the whole ship falls. If the kitchen vents, you eat hard-tack for a month. Simple math. Don't be a hero, be a survivor.

**10. Naval History**
During the infamous Battle of the Six Sails, which maneuver secured victory for the Harmony fleet?
- [x] A) The Crescent Sweep
- [ ] B) The Weeping Node Detonation
- [ ] C) The Ironworks Blockade
- [ ] D) The Aether-Draft Retreat
> **Val's Note:** A. The Crescent Sweep. The textbook loves "The Crescent Sweep." It's on every history exam. Funny thing though—the Battle of the Six Sails never actually happened. The Scrivener Guild completely hallucinated it after bad ether exposure, but nobody wants to rewrite the curriculum.
