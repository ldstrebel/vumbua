## Phase 1: The Morning After & Finding Lucky

### Scene: Post-Ambush → Bed

After the ambush, the party stumbles back to the dorms. They're battered, confused, maybe carrying an unconscious Nstyl fighter. The campus is dead quiet.

**GM Pressure Points:**
- It is **extremely late**. Exams start in a few hours.
- Anyone who tries to investigate Azer tonight instead of sleeping should be warned: *"You can chase this now, or you can sleep. But the exam doesn't care about your night."*
- If they report the ambush to campus security, the response is slow and bureaucratic — forms to fill, statements to give. Time bleeds away.

**Transition:** Handwave the rest of the night. *"You collapse into your bunks. Your head barely hits the pillow before—"*


### Scene: Morning — The Wake-Up Call

> **BULLHORN:** *"ATTENTION ALL FIRST-YEAR CANDIDATES. REPORT TO THE MAIN COURTYARD AT 0800 HOURS FOR TRACK ASSIGNMENT. THIS IS NOT OPTIONAL. STRAGGLERS WILL BE MARKED."*

**Mood:** Harsh morning light. Sore muscles. Not enough sleep. The glamour of the Celestial Lounge feels like a different lifetime. The bruises from the ambush are real.

**Quick Beats:**
- PCs wake up, possibly with Stress still marked from the ambush or sleep deprivation
- Breakfast is "Nutri-Paste" (bland, efficient) unless they make it to Captain's Table (real food, for high ranks)
- Other students are buzzing about the exams — nobody knows about the ambush
- Any NPC who spots their bruises will ask questions

### The Morning After: The "Snooze" Roll

The party collapsed into their bunks at 3:00 AM. The bullhorns go off at 07:00 AM.

**The Mechanic: Resilience Roll (DC 13)**  
Have everyone roll a **Resilience Check** (or Instinct if they are "light sleepers").

- **Critical Success:** You wake up before the horn. You have time for a "Captain's Table" breakfast. **Clear all Stress and gain 1 Hope.**
- **Success (with Hope):** You’re tired, but you make it. You have time for Nutri-Paste. **Clear 1 Stress.**
- **Success (with Fear):** You make it just as the doors are locking. You’re frazzled. **Take 1 Stress.**
- **Failure:** You oversleep. You are hauled out of bed by a Prefect. You miss breakfast and arrive late to "Intro to Resonance." **Take 2 Stress and start the first class with Disadvantage**

### Scene: Finding Lucky (The Extortion)

Before Track Assignment, the party needs to execute their plan: track down [[Lucky]] to get the "study guide" that Valentine sold him. 

**The Setup:**
- The party has Azor's stolen pocket watch and a highly embellished story of Lomi "heroically" breaking the ribs of an attacker.

### Val's Study Guide — Design Philosophy

Val's questions are a "level up" from what might be on the general exam, even though they cover similar core competencies:

1.  **Increased Complexity & Nuance:**
    *   Instead of a "floating island rotating," Valentine's guide presents a "Chrono-Spatial Drift" with flickering charts and temporal eddies, adding layers of temporal distortion to spatial navigation.
    *   A "Node humming in B-Minor" becomes a "Crystalline formation emitting a complex, multi-frequency resonance" causing "unpredictable mutations," demanding a more nuanced bio-harmonic understanding.

2.  **Counter-Intuitive Solutions:**
    *   Where the general exam might test a standard "Synchronized Helix," Valentine's "Entropic Pulse" scenario requires a "controlled energy *drain*" to stabilize, rather than an injection, forcing students to think paradoxically.

3.  **Higher Stakes & Deeper Philosophical/Ethical Challenges:**
    *   "Ether-Mist dissolves your ego" becomes a "Psychic anomaly that fragments your sense of self," requiring a more fundamental anchor than just "The Flaw" or "The Law."
    *   A "resource-negative weed" becomes a "Sentient mineral vein" that feels "immense distress," pushing ethical considerations into truly alien territory.

4.  **Abstracted Practical Application:**
    *   "Manually plotting a non-Euclidean archipelago" evolves into "charting a sub-aetheric current" that "dissipates upon direct observation," demanding predictive intuition over reactive drawing.
    *   "Landing a ship blind" becomes navigating a "Temporal Labyrinth" or "Chronal Eddy" where time itself is erratic, requiring mastery beyond linear perception.

By crafting scenarios that are conceptually related but significantly more intricate, unpredictable, or ethically ambiguous, Valentine's guide forces students to internalize the *principles* of Venture exploration, crisis management, and ethical interaction at a much deeper level. It's designed to make them truly *think* and adapt, rather than just recall information, which aligns perfectly with his character as a playful, high-level mentor.

### Scene: Track Assignment

This is the in-fiction moment where the PCs choose their **Daggerheart class/subclass**, framed as the Academy assigning them to a specialized training track based on their trial performance and personal aptitude.

**Setting:** The Main Courtyard. Rows of brass terminals. Faculty stationed at department tables. [[Dean Isolde Vane]] presiding from a floating podium (as in the Gen-Ed exam from S2).

**How it works:**
1. Students approach a terminal and receive their **recommended track** based on trial scores
2. They can **accept** the recommendation or **petition** for a different track (requires justification)
3. The party already proved themselves in Gen-Ed — they have some flexibility

### Player Engagement

- **Let players discuss in-character** — this is a big life decision for their characters. Don't rush it.
- **NPCs weigh in** — Valentine might recommend his own track smugly. Ember might comment. Cassius is stressed about it.
- **The tracks create social dynamics** — PCs in the same track will share classes; PCs in different tracks will have separate adventures.
- **The ambush aftermath lingers** — did anyone report it? Are campus authorities asking questions? Does Azer's scheme become relevant here?

> [!WARNING]
> **Pacing check:** Phase 3 could easily absorb an entire session if players want to explore the class selection deeply. Consider whether this is a quick montage or a full scene depending on table energy.

### 2. The First Week Montage:


To make the first day feel truly overwhelming, you want to lean into the **sensory overload** and the **brutal math** of the Academy. Here is a narrative kickoff and a breakdown of the "Gen-Ed Grind" to set the tone.

### 1. The Narrative Kickoff: The Sea of Grey

> "The dorm doors hiss open at 07:30, and the silence of the night is obliterated as seventy thousand students pouring out of the residential blocks like a grey tide. The air smells of ozone, cheap caffeine-sticks, and the collective sweat of seventy thousand people who didn't sleep enough. 
> 
> High above, the **Loom Terminals** aren't showing schedules anymore; they’re showing a countdown. **'70,000 CANDIDATES. 50,000 SLOTS. 96 HOURS REMAINING.'** Every person you bump into in the hallway isn't just a classmate; they are a statistical obstacle. The 'Great Filter' has begun."

### 2. The Lecture Hall: "The Echo Chamber"

The first class isn't in a room; it’s in **Hangar 12**, a repurposed airship dry-dock that holds 5,000 students at a time. 

- **The Visual:** Thousands of glowing tablets and floating holographic displays. The air is cold, and the acoustics are so sharp you can hear a stylus drop three tiers down.
- **The Professor:** **[[professor-kante|Professor Kante]]** (Resonance Theory) or **Magister Vane-Smythe** (Aetheric History). They don't use a microphone; their voice is amplified by a Resonance-Pillar that makes their words vibrate in your very marrow.
- **The Vibe:** "Look to your left. Look to your right. By Friday, one of you will be packing your bags for the surface. The Academy does not fail students; students fail to resonate."

### 3. The Montage: The "Gen-Ed" Gauntlet

Use these "flashes" to describe the first three days (Monday–Wednesday morning). Ask the players which of these they are struggling with most.

- **Subject: Aetheric Ballistics & Logistics**
    - **Professor:** **Instructor Hallow.** A veteran with a prosthetic arm made of reclaimed brass.
    - **The Grind:** Calculating the wind-shear of a falling supply crate from 30,000 feet while under simulated "Static-Storm" conditions. The math is relentless.
- **Subject: The History of the Shatter**
    - **Professor:** **Archivist Thorne.** A man who looks like he’s made of old parchment.
    - **The Grind:** Memorizing the names of the 12 Founding Houses and the exact timestamp of the "Great Desync." If you miss a date by a second, your terminal flashes red, and your "Class Rank" drops in real-time on the public leaderboard.
- **Subject: Resonance Biology**
    - **Professor:** **Doctor Aris.** A woman who speaks in rapid-fire medical jargon.
    - **The Grind:** Dissecting "Resonance-Leeches" to understand how they drain a pilot's focus. The smell of formaldehyde and static-fluid is nauseating.
- **Subject: Survival Ethics**
    - **Professor:** **Dean Isolde Vane** (via pre-recorded hologram).
    - **The Grind:** A series of "No-Win" scenarios. *“Your rig is failing. You have one oxygen canister. Your squadmate is unconscious. Do you stay, or do you salvage their battery to reach the extraction point?”*

---

### 4. Daggerheart Mechanic: The "Focus" Rewards (Expanded)

As they go through this montage, give them their "First Week Reward" based on which class they prioritized.

- **If they focused on Ballistics (Instructor Hallow):**
    - **Reward:** **"Lead the Shot" (Experience).** Once per session, you can add your Agility or Precision to a damage roll as you find the "perfect arc."
- **If they focused on History (Archivist Thorne):**
    - **Reward:** **"Lore-Hunter" (Utility).** You gain a "Memory Fragment." You can spend this to automatically know one piece of obscure lore about a location or NPC without rolling.
- **If they focused on Biology (Doctor Aris):**
    - **Reward:** **"Adrenaline Surge" (Passive).** The first time you take a "Major" or "Severe" wound, you gain 2 Hope as your body's resonance kicks into overdrive.
- **If they focused on Ethics (Dean Vane):**
    - **Reward:** **"Cold Resolve" (Passive).** You can spend 1 Hope to ignore the "Frightened" or "Stunned" condition for one turn.

### Phase 2: The Interrogation Room & The RR Merch

**Scene: Lucky's Supply Room**
The "interrogation chamber" is actually Lucky's private storeroom—a cluttered, dimly lit bunker filled with crates of "acquired" goods, strange aetheric tools, and contraband. [[Sarge]] is there, leaning against a crate with a heavy sigh, acting as Lucky's muscle (though mostly just keeping an eye on him out of obligation to his late parents).

**The Interrogation:**
- Lucky presses the trio, especially [[Iggy]], about what they're *really* doing at the Academy and how they got the gold receipt.
- The interrogation is tense but mostly just a high-pressure sales tactic to measure their worth.

**The Interruption:**
- About halfway through the interrogation, there's a frantic, rhythmic knock at the storeroom door.
- It's **Dash**, an eccentric, fast-talking student who claims to have an "in" for merch for the **"rr"** (codeword for the Resonance Race).
- This completely derails Lucky. The prospect of exclusive Resonance Race merchandise gets him so excited that he abandons his intense questioning. He tells the party they "owe him" and rushes out to make the deal, leaving Sarge to awkwardly usher the PCs out.
