+### Val's Study Guide ΓÇö Design Philosophy
+
+Val's questions are a "level up" from what might be on the general exam, even though they cover similar core competencies:
+
+1.  **Increased Complexity & Nuance:**
+    *   Instead of a "floating island rotating," Valentine's guide presents a "Chrono-Spatial Drift" with flickering charts and temporal eddies, adding layers of temporal distortion to spatial navigation.
+    *   A "Node humming in B-Minor" becomes a "Crystalline formation emitting a complex, multi-frequency resonance" causing "unpredictable mutations," demanding a more nuanced bio-harmonic understanding.
+
+2.  **Counter-Intuitive Solutions:**
+    *   Where the general exam might test a standard "Synchronized Helix," Valentine's "Entropic Pulse" scenario requires a "controlled energy *drain*" to stabilize, rather than an injection, forcing students to think paradoxically.
+
+3.  **Higher Stakes & Deeper Philosophical/Ethical Challenges:**
+    *   "Ether-Mist dissolves your ego" becomes a "Psychic anomaly that fragments your sense of self," requiring a more fundamental anchor than just "The Flaw" or "The Law."
+    *   A "resource-negative weed" becomes a "Sentient mineral vein" that feels "immense distress," pushing ethical considerations into truly alien territory.
+
+4.  **Abstracted Practical Application:**
+    *   "Manually plotting a non-Euclidean archipelago" evolves into "charting a sub-aetheric current" that "dissipates upon direct observation," demanding predictive intuition over reactive drawing.
+    *   "Landing a ship blind" becomes navigating a "Temporal Labyrinth" or "Chronal Eddy" where time itself is erratic, requiring mastery beyond linear perception.
+
+By crafting scenarios that are conceptually related but significantly more intricate, unpredictable, or ethically ambiguous, Valentine's guide forces students to internalize the *principles* of Venture exploration, crisis management, and ethical interaction at a much deeper level. It's designed to make them truly *think* and adapt, rather than just recall information, which aligns perfectly with his character as a playful, high-level mentor.

+4. **The Teller reveals the truth:** Was it real or a lie?
+5. **Compare rolls after the reveal:**
+   - If your Instinct roll **ΓëÑ the Teller's Presence roll**, you guessed correctly. Earn a **Hope token.**

+
+- The GM doesn't need to manage a guessing phase ΓÇö just let everyone roll, reveal, then narrate retroactively.
+- This is how Daggerheart handles many social situations: roll first, narrate the fiction to match.
+- It lets players practice **collaborative storytelling** ΓÇö they build the scene after the dice tell them the outcome.
+- **Bonus:** Players learn things about each other's backstories through the truths and lies. This is *actual character development* disguised as a drinking game.

+### Azor's Scheme

+**The Setup:** Tus and Zyykl both **failed out of the first round of entrance exams.** They're devastated ΓÇö they came all the way from [[Nstyl]] to prove themselves, and they believed in the Academy more than anything.

+**The Real Plan:** Azor figured that the only students dumb enough to be out this late ΓÇö the night before the most important week of most people's lives ΓÇö would be the **rich and drunk ones.** Prudent students would be asleep. -### ≡ƒâÅ Game 2: "Truths & Treachery" *(Truth-or-Lie Social Game)*
-
-**What it teaches:** Contested rolls, how different stats work against each other, retcon narration.
-
-**The Drink:** Same Void Shots. **You take a shot (mark Stress) if you guess wrong.**
-
-#### Rules
-
-1. One player is the **Teller.** They tell the table something about themselves, their clan, their past ΓÇö **either completely true or a total lie.**
-2. The Teller makes a **Presence roll** (stat + 2d12) ΓÇö this is their "poker face" score. *They do NOT reveal the number yet.*
-3. Every other player makes an **Instinct roll** (stat + 2d12) ΓÇö this is their "read" on the Teller.
-4. **The Teller reveals the truth:** Was it real or a lie?
-5. **Compare rolls after the reveal:**
-   - If your Instinct roll **ΓëÑ the Teller's Presence roll**, you guessed correctly. Earn a **Hope token.**
-   - If your Instinct roll **< the Teller's Presence roll**, you guessed wrong. **Take a shot. Mark Stress.**
-6. **Retcon the guesses:** After the reveal, players narrate what their character's reaction was *in the moment*. The GM asks: *"So [PC], when they said that ΓÇö what did you think? Did you buy it?"* Players whose rolls succeeded get to say they saw through it; players who failed get to describe being fooled.
-
-#### Why the Retcon Works
-
-- The GM doesn't need to manage a guessing phase ΓÇö just let everyone roll, reveal, then narrate retroactively.
-- This is how Daggerheart handles many social situations: roll first, narrate the fiction to match.
-- It lets players practice **collaborative storytelling** ΓÇö they build the scene after the dice tell them the outcome.
-- **Bonus:** Players learn things about each other's backstories through the truths and lies. This is *actual character development* disguised as a drinking game.
-
-#### GM Notes
-
-- Val and Ember go first to demonstrate the format. Then PCs take turns.
-- Encourage players to use **real backstory** for their truths ΓÇö this is free character-building.
-- NPC statements are pre-written below ΓÇö pick 2-3 for each NPC depending on time.
-- **Ember is taking mental notes for Valerius's radio show.** Anything juicy the PCs reveal may appear on air later.
-
-#### How the NPCs Play
-
-**Valentine ΓÇö The Smooth Operator**
-- Val treats T&T like a performance. He leans back, steeples his fingers, and delivers each statement like a theatre monologue.
-- **High Presence.** He's *genuinely good at lying* ΓÇö smooth voice, steady eye contact, confident rhythm. His poker face is championship-level.
-- **Strategically vulnerable.** He mixes real emotional truths with outrageous lies, making it hard to tell which is which. He *wants* people to underestimate his sincerity.
-- When he reads others, he does it with a raised eyebrow and a knowing smile: *"Interesting. Very interesting."*
-- **If caught in a lie:** Takes the shot gracefully. *"Well played. I almost had you."*
-- **If his truth is doubted:** Genuine flash of hurt he quickly masks. A crack in the armour.
-
-**Ember ΓÇö The Intensity**
-- Ember treats T&T like an interrogation. She leans forward, studies faces, barely blinks.
-- **Low Presence, high Instinct.** She's bad at lying (too intense, too earnest) but *excellent* at reading others. She stares right through people.
-- When someone tells their statement, Ember is visibly analysing ΓÇö head tilted, eyes narrowed. She looks like she's trying to decode them.
-- **Trying to fit in.** She wants to be fun and casual about it, but she can't help being competitive. She'll say something like *"This is such a fun game!"* with the energy of someone filing a police report.
-- **If she guesses wrong:** Takes the shot fast, then immediately explains her reasoning to nobody: *"No, but see, the way you said it ΓÇö the cadence wasΓÇö"*
-- **When it's her turn to tell:** She gets nervous. These truths cut close to home. Her voice drops. She stops performing.

-## Phase 2: The Ambush
-
-### Setting
-
-**Location:** A dark stretch of campus between the Celestial Lounge and the dorms ΓÇö perhaps an unlit courtyard or maintenance corridor. Late at night, few witnesses.
-
-**Time:** After the PCs leave the Celestial Lounge. The campus is quiet, most students already in bed before exams.
-
-**Mood:** Sudden shift from warm social energy to cold, tense danger. Shadows, silence, then violence.
-
----
-
-### The Situation
-
-The PCs are ambushed by **Tus** and **Zyykl**, two burly young men from [[Nstyl]] ΓÇö the Silent Silt city where sound is physically impossible and everyone communicates through sign language. They don't speak; they *can't* speak. They just attack.
-
-But Tus and Zyykl aren't the real threat ΓÇö they've been **manipulated** by **Azor**, a street-smart schemer from [[Settika]] who used [[Prism Falls]] water to convince them this was their only shot at redemption.
-
----
-
-### Azor's Scheme
-
-**The Setup:** Tus and Zyykl both **failed out of the first round of entrance exams.** They're devastated ΓÇö they came all the way from [[Nstyl]] to prove themselves, and they believed in the Academy more than anything.
-
-**The Con:** Azor tracked them down and used water from [[Prism Falls]] (Settika's node resource ΓÇö water that reveals the true nature of whatever it touches). He told them:
-
-> *"I know what you really are. The water showed me. You're Exploranauts ΓÇö real ones. The test was wrong. All you need is a receipt from a current student, and you can retake the exam. The Academy will see they failed the wrong people."*
-
-Tus and Zyykl **believe in the power of the Prism Falls**. Back in [[Nstyl]], Settika's water is respected, almost sacred. If the water says they're Exploranauts, they must be. They're naive, earnest, and desperate.
-
-**The Truth:** Azor knows this won't work. The [[The Loom|Loom]] receipts aren't transferable IDs ΓÇö they're point-in-time documents. The Loom recognises students based on how they interact with it, not by a piece of paper. Azor doesn't care about helping Tus and Zyykl. He's a mugger, plain and simple.
-
-**The Real Plan:** Azor figured that the only students dumb enough to be out this late ΓÇö the night before the most important week of most people's lives ΓÇö would be the **rich and drunk ones.** Prudent students would be asleep. So he waits in the shadows, lets Tus and Zyykl do the dangerous work, and plans to take whatever valuables fall out of pockets during the scuffle.
-
----
-
-### Key NPCs
-
-#### Azor ΓÇö The Man from Settika
-
-A world-wise, street-smart manipulator from [[Settika]]. He knows how things work ΓÇö Loom receipts, Academy bureaucracy, Prism Falls water. He uses that knowledge to exploit people who don't.
-
-**Key traits:**
-- **Sneaky and manipulative** ΓÇö operates through proxies, never gets his hands dirty.
-- **Not from a clan** ΓÇö he's a city kid from [[Settika]], where the Prism Falls resource is just another tool.
-- **Knows the con doesn't work** ΓÇö he told Tus and Zyykl what they wanted to hear. The receipts are worthless.
-- **Actual goal:** Mug rich students and disappear before morning.
-
-**During the fight:**
-- Azor is **watching from the shadows**, not engaging directly. He lets Tus and Zyykl take the risk.
-- If the fight goes badly (PCs are too strong), he may slip away entirely ΓÇö a loose thread for later.
-- If the PCs are losing, he may step in to grab a crystal battery or coin purse.
-
-#### Tus & Zyykl ΓÇö The Nstyl Brothers
-
-Two large, powerful young men from [[Nstyl]], the city with [[harmony-nodes#13 Nstyl|Silent Silt]] ΓÇö where all sound is absorbed and everyone communicates through sign language. They will not speak during the encounter because they physically cannot ΓÇö spoken language doesn't exist in their world.
-
-**Key traits:**
-- **Sign language only** ΓÇö the PCs can't reason with them verbally. A PC who knows sign language could potentially communicate.
-- **Names are gestures, not words** ΓÇö "Tus" and "Zyykl" are outsider approximations of their hand-sign names. Different people pronounce them differently. (See [[harmony-nodes#13 Nstyl|Nstyl naming conventions]].)
-- **Naive, not evil** ΓÇö they genuinely believe they're supposed to be Academy students. Azor used Prism Falls water to convince them. They think they're *reclaiming* something that was wrongly taken.
-- **Failed the entrance exam** ΓÇö they're heartbroken about it. This isn't rage; it's desperation.
-- **Burly and strong** ΓÇö these are big boys. Farm-strong from the Nstyl quarries. Built for labour, not subtlety.
-- **If defeated:** They seem confused and ashamed. Once the adrenaline fades, they realise something is wrong. If a PC can communicate in sign language, Tus will try to explain ΓÇö *"The water said we were real. The man said the paper would prove it."*

+The PCs are ambushed by **Tus** and **Zyykl**, two burly young men from [[Nstyl]] ΓÇö the Silent Silt city where sound is physically impossible and everyone communicates through sign language. They don't speak; they *can't* speak. They just attack.
+
+But Tus and Zyykl aren't the real threat ΓÇö they've been **manipulated** by **Azor**, a street-smart schemer from [[Settika]] who used [[Prism Falls]] water to convince them this was their only shot at redemption.
+
+---
+
+### Azor's Scheme
+
+**The Setup:** Tus and Zyykl both **failed out of the first round of entrance exams.** They're devastated ΓÇö they came all the way from [[Nstyl]] to prove themselves, and they believed in the Academy more than anything.
+
+**The Con:** Azor tracked them down and used water from [[Prism Falls]] (Settika's node resource ΓÇö water that reveals the true nature of whatever it touches). He told them:
+
+> *"I know what you really are. The water showed me. You're Exploranauts ΓÇö real ones. The test was wrong. All you need is a receipt from a current student, and you can retake the exam. The Academy will see they failed the wrong people."*
+
+Tus and Zyykl **believe in the power of the Prism Falls**. Back in [[Nstyl]], Settika's water is respected, almost sacred. If the water says they're Exploranauts, they must be. They're naive, earnest, and desperate.
+
+**The Truth:** Azor knows this won't work. The [[The Loom|Loom]] receipts aren't transferable IDs ΓÇö they're point-in-time documents. The Loom recognises students based on how they interact with it, not by a piece of paper. Azor doesn't care about helping Tus and Zyykl. He's a mugger, plain and simple.
+
+**The Real Plan:** Azor figured that the only students dumb enough to be out this late ΓÇö the night before the most important week of most people's lives ΓÇö would be the **rich and drunk ones.** Prudent students would be asleep. So he waits in the shadows, lets Tus and Zyykl do the dangerous work, and plans to take whatever valuables fall out of pockets during the scuffle.
+

+### Key NPCs
+
+#### Azor ΓÇö The Man from Settika
+
+A world-wise, street-smart manipulator from [[Settika]]. He knows how things work ΓÇö Loom receipts, Academy bureaucracy, Prism Falls water. He uses that knowledge to exploit people who don't.
+
+**Key traits:**
+- **Sneaky and manipulative** ΓÇö operates through proxies, never gets his hands dirty.
+- **Not from a clan** ΓÇö he's a city kid from [[Settika]], where the Prism Falls resource is just another tool.
+- **Knows the con doesn't work** ΓÇö he told Tus and Zyykl what they wanted to hear. The receipts are worthless.
+- **Actual goal:** Mug rich students and disappear before morning.
+
+**During the fight:**
+- Azor is **watching from the shadows**, not engaging directly. He lets Tus and Zyykl take the risk.
+- If the fight goes badly (PCs are too strong), he may slip away entirely ΓÇö a loose thread for later.
+- If the PCs are losing, he may step in to grab a crystal battery or coin purse.
+
+#### Tus & Zyykl ΓÇö The Nstyl Brothers
+
+Two large, powerful young men from [[Nstyl]], the city with [[harmony-nodes#13 Nstyl|Silent Silt]] ΓÇö where all sound is absorbed and everyone communicates through sign language. They will not speak during the encounter because they physically cannot ΓÇö spoken language doesn't exist in their world.
+
+**Key traits:**
+- **Sign language only** ΓÇö the PCs can't reason with them verbally. A PC who knows sign language could potentially communicate.
+- **Names are gestures, not words** ΓÇö "Tus" and "Zyykl" are outsider approximations of their hand-sign names. Different people pronounce them differently. (See [[harmony-nodes#13 Nstyl|Nstyl naming conventions]].)
+- **Naive, not evil** ΓÇö they genuinely believe they're supposed to be Academy students. Azor used Prism Falls water to convince them. They think they're *reclaiming* something that was wrongly taken.
+- **Failed the entrance exam** ΓÇö they're heartbroken about it. This isn't rage; it's desperation.
+- **Burly and strong** ΓÇö these are big boys. Farm-strong from the Nstyl quarries. Built for labour, not subtlety.
+- **If defeated:** They seem confused and ashamed. Once the adrenaline fades, they realise something is wrong. If a PC can communicate in sign language, Tus will try to explain ΓÇö *"The water said we were real. The man said the paper would prove it."*

+1. **The Walk Home** ΓÇö PCs leave the Celestial Lounge, tired, maybe laughing about Val's antics. Describe the campus growing quiet and dark.
