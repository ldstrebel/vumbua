# The First Vumbua Circuit-Run: Full Race Simulation

> **GM Reference** — This is the complete play-by-play for the opening [[circuit-run|Circuit-Run]] at [[Vumbua Academy]]. Use it to run the race as a vivid spectacle, or adapt it scene-by-scene based on player interaction. The session-facing overview lives in [[sessions/planning/Session 4 plan|Session 4 Plan]].
>
> **How to use this document:** Each Phase opens with an *atmosphere paragraph* — read it aloud or paraphrase it to your players to set the scene. The sync tables are reference material for you, not read-aloud text. The *Expanded Moment* sections are the cinematic beats you narrate in detail. The `> GM Beat:` callouts are private director's notes.

## Phase 0 — Certification Lock

### Atmosphere

The basin smells like hot metal and stone dust. Twenty thousand people have been filing into the amphitheaters since before dawn, and the south rim bleacher-walls are already vibrating — not from crowd noise, but from the machinery beneath: the Loom-Pulse mechanisms cycling through their pre-race alignment sequence, deep subterranean thrumming that spectators feel in their teeth more than their ears.

The rigs are lined up along the launch gantries in weight-class order, and the contrast is almost absurd. At one end, Stinger's razor hover-bike sits low and whisper-thin, almost invisible against the stone lip of the rim. It looks less like a racing vehicle and more like a weapon someone forgot to load. At the other end, Goliath is being argued at by three marshals who cannot agree on whether its mass exceeds the super-heavy tolerance limit. The land-ship does not appear to notice this conversation.

Down in the south stands, vendors are hawking Shatter Stamper pennants in the deep burgundy and hammered-iron grey of The Ironclad's hull colors. They're selling fast. Calculus Prime has published their pre-race probability breakdown in the Academy's morning bulletin, and the 68.4% figure has settled over the crowd like a comfortable blanket. Most people are not here to be surprised.

Most people.

**T-24 hours:** Officials seal declared draw bands and launch classes.

- **Controversy:** Pudge's Gryphon-Hybrid draw is disputed. The gyro-boat itself rates as Light class, but the gryphon's metabolic drive adds effective mass. Officials rule it enters as **Experimental class**, with a Heavy launch penalty offset by the light hull. Final CD lands at 195 — equal to Marble Wall on paper, but for completely different reasons.
- **Calculus Prime** (Squad 04) projects final odds from the high bleachers: *"Shatter Stamper, 68.4%. Pudge, 3.1%. Dancer & Fabian, 0.8% — rounding error."* The crowd around them laughs. Calculus Prime does not.
- **Lucky** is already circulating in the bleachers, selling *Shatter Stamper* flags and taking bets. He has been here since the gantry doors opened. He will not sit down once during this race.

## Phase 1 — The Drop

### Atmosphere

The opening horn is not a single note. It is an ascending chord — three brass instruments mounted above the south gate firing in sequence, each a fifth higher than the last — and by the third note the entire basin is already roaring. The Rim-Tracker trolley lurches to its start position. The Gilded Zephyrs adjust altitude. The Bleacher-Wall crowd surges forward as one.

The rigs hang on their launch couplers over open air. Below them, the basin floor drops forty meters to raw terrain: canyon rock and petrified wood and the first low glimmer of dry sea-bed clay catching the morning light. It is a long way down even for the lightest rig. For Goliath, it is unconscionable.

**Opening Horn fires. The basin erupts.**

The rigs are slung or dropped from the Southern Rim in weight-class order, lightest first.

### Launch Sequence

| Order | Rig | Launch Style | Initial Trajectory |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1st | Stinger | Slung hard east | Hard right toward Friction Needles canyon |
| 2nd | Dancer & Fabian | Controlled sail arc | Wide graceful arc toward Whetstone shelf |
| 3rd | Sail & Stun | Aggressive burn | Hard south toward Live Soil plain |
| 4th | Pudge | Thermal catch | NORTH — gryphon banks immediately, skipping the opening band entirely. Every spectator gasps. |
| 5th–6th | Shatter Stamper / Marble Wall | Heavy drop | Canyon floor impact; The Ironclad shakes the ground |
| Last | Goliath | Controlled descent | Barely mobile; nearly crushes Stinger on the way down |

### The Drop — Expanded

Stinger goes first and goes *sideways*. The sling mechanism catches the hover-bike at an almost horizontal angle, and the razor frame skips across the rim wind like a stone on water before correcting into a hard banking dive toward the eastern canyon mouth. For a three-second window, the pilot is effectively parallel to the basin wall. The crowd on the east bleachers loses their minds.

Dancer & Fabian follow — a Glass-Sail launch is an art form, slow enough to look like a mistake at first. The two-person crew work in unison from the moment the coupler releases: one on hull trim, one on the primary sail. Their arc out from the south rim is genuinely beautiful, a wide parabola that takes them low and slow toward the Whetstone shelf. Nobody in the stands is cheering for them. Nobody is booing either. The crowd is simply watches, uncertain what to make of a racing sailboat that's taking its time.

Then Pudge hits the air, and the crowd does something unusual: it goes silent for a full three seconds.

The Gryphon-Hybrid doesn't drop. The launch coupler releases and the gryphon *catches the thermal*. Instead of falling toward the south launch canyons where every other rig has gone, the creature banks hard — a 90-degree turn into the upwelling from the basin's north volcanic shelf — and climbs. The gyro-boat hull swings beneath it like a pendulum, stabilizing as the gryphon levels out at altitude and begins gliding north. By the time the crowd processes what it's watching, Pudge is already three hundred meters above the basin floor and moving in exactly the wrong direction.

Someone in the Bleacher-Walls shouts *"WHAT IS THAT IDIOT DOING?!"* and the crowd exhales into laughter, tension releasing all at once.

The heavy rigs fall last, and they fall like what they are. The Ironclad hits the canyon floor and sends a crack resonating through the rock — spectators on the lower bleachers feel it in their boots. Marble Wall touches down a hundred meters south and does not slow down. It is already turning. Not toward a spire. Toward the razor hover-bike that disappeared into the eastern canyon thirty seconds ago.

> **GM Beat:** When Pudge banks north, have someone in the bleachers shout *"What is that IDIOT doing?!"* The spectators near the party don't understand the strategy yet. Professor Kante, watching from the North Research Deck, is smiling.

## Phase 2 — First Sync Window (South Launch Canyons)

### Atmosphere

The south canyon band is chaos in the way that only organized chaos can be: every rig moving with intent, nothing intersecting yet, but the vectors are drawing toward each other at speed. The canyon walls are close enough here that vehicle noise echoes and compounds — the Ironclad's engine roar bouncing off petrified rock until it sounds like the canyon itself is angry. Dust rises in rooster-tails behind the lighter rigs. The first three Spires are visible — their metallic pillar-forms caught the dawn light before the Drop even started, and every pilot has had them memorized since Certification Lock.

**Available Spires:** Friction Needles (30 CU, 2 charges), Whetstone (30 CU, 2 charges), Live Soil (35 CU, 2 charges)

### Marble Wall's First Hunt — Expanded

Marble Wall hits its first Spire and keeps running. The Scrivener booth flags the missed sync. The crowd murmurs — everyone can see The Bastion passing within twenty meters of the Whetstone spire and simply not stopping.

Then the Rim-Tracker camera tracks where The Bastion *is* heading, and the murmur turns to a low drone of discomfort.

Stinger is in the eastern canyon, Slipline still active from the Friction Needles sync, the hover-bike threading between petrified trunks that have been here since before Harmony existed. CD is down to 15 — one more reasonable node and Stinger has cleared deficit entirely. The pilot is ninety seconds away from routing toward the center.

Marble Wall doesn't know Stinger's exact CD. It doesn't need to. The math is visible to anyone watching the scoreboard: smallest rig, fastest sync, already nearly clear. The Bastion's strategy has nothing to do with the Grand Resonator and everything to do with ensuring there are fewer competitors than there were at the start.

The hover-bike tries to use Slipline to sprint clear — but both Whetstone charges are already depleted. There's no second speed-boon waiting in the south band. Stinger is quick, but The Bastion's bulk is designed to close in a straight canyon where the walls prevent the evasion the open terrain would allow. The Bastion shadows the hover-bike through two turns. Waits. The pilot knows what's coming and can't stop it.

> **Spectator Reaction:** The Bleacher-Walls go strangely quiet as the Bastion's bulk enters the canyon behind the razor hover-bike. Everyone senses what's coming.

## Phase 3 — Loom-Pulse 1 (West Petrified Forest / Mizizi Simulation)

### Atmosphere

The first horn fires with no warning — a single deep tone that rolls across the basin and sends a visible shiver through the crowd. Every spectator who's attended a Circuit-Run before knows what it means. Newer attendees learn quickly: a full column of metallic Spire infrastructure erupts from the petrified forest floor to the west, launched from underground channels on hydraulic rams, arriving at surface level with a sound like a cannon volley. Three pillars, each forty feet tall, gleaming in the morning sun in the middle of stone forest that has stood unchanged for centuries.

The west band terrain is the reason this year's race is already being called historic. The petrified forest simulation is visually identical to the actual Mizizi Forest territory — every tree a massive stone column, the fossilized canopy overhead creating corridors of bone-colored light between pillar-trunks as wide as houses. It looks less like terrain and more like the inside of a cathedral that has been dead a very long time.

For Britt and Aggie, if they're watching from the bleachers, this moment may be worth pausing.

**The first Loom-Pulse horn fires. New spires erupt from the petrified forest floor.**

**Available Spires:** Soft Forge (45 CU, 2 charges), Trail Mark (20 CU, 2 charges), Pulse Sense (20 CU, 2 charges)

### Sync Ledger

| Team | Node Hit | CU | CD Before → After | Boon Gained | Notes |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Stinger | Soft Forge | 45 | 15 → **−30** | Rapid Refit | **CD ≤ 0. Legal for Grand Resonator.** Routing center now. |
| Dancer & Fabian | Trail Mark | 20 | 25 → **5** | Trail Mark | Plants a route marker through the dry canyon back to center. |
| Sail & Stun | Pulse Sense | 20 | 60 → **40** | Pulse Sense | Now knows next spires emerge in the north and east bands. Banks east. |
| Shatter Stamper | Soft Forge (2nd charge) | 45 | 135 → **90** | Rapid Refit | Patches impact damage from the Drop. Now entering contention range. |
| Marble Wall | Pulse Sense (2nd charge) | 20 | 195 → **175** | Pulse Sense | Grabbed in passing. They know the next bands, too. Don't care. |
| Pudge | *(none)* | — | 195 → **195** | — | Over the treetops. The Scrivener booth is flagging them as a DNF risk. |

### The Marble Wall vs Stinger Confrontation — Expanded

Stinger routes immediately for the petrified forest. The Soft Forge spire is visible at the edge of the first stone corridor and the hover-bike's intercept angle is clean — thirty seconds at Slipline speed. The Harvest-Prong makes contact. CD: 15 → **negative thirty**. The pilot doesn't celebrate. They're already turning south-center, routing for the Grand Resonator. They just need to get there.

The problem: the petrified forest is not favorable terrain for a razor hover-bike. The stone pillars interrupt the slipstream that Stinger's momentum depends on, and the Bastion knows the forest layout from the same pre-race scans every team used at Certification Lock. Marble Wall enters from the south canyon, driving between trunks with the casual confidence of a vehicle that is approximately the same width as the corridor it's running in.

Stinger sees it. Hard dodge left — but The Bastion's navigator fires its kinetic dampeners sideways: a short-range gravity pulse, directional, designed for exactly this moment. The hover-bike's lift mechanism stutters. Not fails — *stutters*. For half a second the razor frame drops and reorients. Half a second is enough.

The collision is not a head-on impact. It's more like being swatted. The Bastion's angled fender clips the hover-bike's left vane and sends it cartwheeling into a petrified trunk. The Harvest-Prong shears off on impact. The crash webbing deploys with a sound like a hammer hitting fabric, and the flare dye blooms — orange, bright, immediate — across thirty square meters of stone bark.

The hover-bike is still. The pilot is moving. Shaken, teeth rattled loose-feeling in their skull, but the webbing caught them and the dead-switch cut power before the battery could rupture.

Two minutes later, a rope team slides from the west rim. Seven minutes after that, the extraction cart is heading for the medical bay. The **orange flag** over the western sector goes up and stays up.

Marble Wall pivots. CD: 175. It cannot discharge. But its tactical goal was never the Resonator. The Bastion moves south-center toward the Grand Resonator approach lane, and it moves like something that has decided to own property.

> **GM Beat:** If Cassius Thorne is visible in the bleachers, he's shouting "YES! THAT'S STRATEGY!" Iron-Jaw Jax is shaking the railing. Lucky is frantically swapping his flags.

> **Player Touchpoint — Mizizi Terrain (Britt / Aggie):** The west petrified forest simulation is visually accurate to what the Mizizi territory actually looks like — stone columns, fossilized canopy, bone-colored light through dead pillar-trunks. If either PC watches the race action in this sector, it might hit unexpectedly hard. Home, rendered as a sporting spectacle. *Rill is somewhere in the maintenance pits on the basin floor, calibrating a battery charge readout. They haven't looked up at the forest simulation once.*

## Phase 4 — Loom-Pulse 2 (North Open Flats and East Wet Shelf)

### Atmosphere

The second horn fires and the race opens up. The north flat band is all sky and scale — where the south canyons were tight and enclosed, the north flats stretch into a wide volcanic plain geography, the Ash-Blood terrain slice running along the northern edge. Heat shimmer rises from the scored rock. Flash-arc hazards dot the exposed geology — miniature lightning events sparked by the resonance-active substrate. And rising from the flat, responding to the Loom-Pulse, two spires that had been waiting under the surface: the Lift Stone and Umber Crystal, both gleaming, both contested between every team that was smart enough to run north.

Only one team ran north.

**The second Loom-Pulse horn fires. Northern and eastern spires erupt.**

**North Open Flats:** Lift Stone (40 CU, 2 charges), Umber Crystal (70 CU, 2 charges), Chime Spire not yet available (held for Loom-Pulse 3)
**East Wet Shelf:** Dew Crystal (25 CU, 2 charges), Breathable Algae (20 CU, 2 charges), Prism Falls (30 CU, 2 charges)
**Mid-Basin (emerging):** Speaking Stone (25 CU, 2 charges), Silent Silt (20 CU, 2 charges), Snow Sand (15 CU, 2 charges)

### Pudge's North Flats Sequence — Expanded

For forty minutes, the Scrivener booth has been tracking the Gryphon-Hybrid as a statistically irrelevant DNF candidate. The probability display still shows **3.1%**. The crowd in the north viewing section — the smallest and least prestigious stand — has been watching a gryphon circle.

Now the Loom-Pulse fires and the north flats bloom.

The Lift Stone spire erupts directly in the Gryphon-Hybrid's glide path, as if placed there by someone who understood exactly what Pudge was doing. The gryphon's banking angle shifts. The hull swings on its rigging. Pudge's Harvest-Prong arm extends from the hull cradle.

| Sync | Node | CU | CD | Boon | Notes |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|
| 1st | Lift Stone | 40 | 195 → **155** | Loadlight | Gryphon-hybrid mass halved. Sudden acceleration surge. Spectacular banking visible from every tier. |
| 2nd | Umber Crystal | 70 | 155 → **85** | Deficit Shear | **Deficit Shear bonus:** −15 additional → CD: **70**. Pudge is suddenly viable. |

The Loadlight boon fires and the Gryphon-Hybrid's registered mass drops by half in the calculation systems. On the north viewing deck, spectators see the *physical* effect before the scoreboard updates: the gryphon's wingbeats become effortless. The animal was already powerful — now it looks like it's flying through a different atmosphere than everything else in the basin. The hull swings wide on a banking turn and comes back faster.

Then Pudge hits Umber Crystal, and the crowd on the north deck sees the scoreboard number drop by eighty-five.

The Deficit Shear boon fires its bonus. **CD: 70.** In a race where the favorites are still north of 50 CU from viability, a team that was written off as a joke is suddenly one major node away from challenging.

> **Professor Kante's Reaction:** The tortoise professor has been watching from the North Research Deck for the entire race, silent and clinical. When Pudge hits Umber Crystal, Kante closes their notebook, sets it down, and leans forward with both hands on the railing. The researcher beside them whispers, *"Is it going to be enough?"* Kante: *"They're not done."*

> **Player Touchpoint — The Gryphon's Physics (Iggy):** Every student who paid attention in Kante's class is watching Pudge's gryphon with new eyes. The Loadlight boon halves the rig's effective mass — but the gryphon's own mass is organic, not rated. Technically the gryphon itself is running on biological metabolism, not stored connection. Iggy, if watching through binoculars, has the information to ask: *"If the gryphon is outside the umber gutter's dead zone... does it add back ambient ether that the gutter is trying to suppress?"* This is the exact question Kante is asking from the north rim.

## Phase 5 — The Convergence

### Atmosphere

Three teams are viable. The race has compressed, as it always does in the final third — the open geography of the basin's outer bands has funneled toward the center, where the Grand Resonator pylon stands at the exact geographic midpoint, an obsidian column approximately twelve meters tall, inert and waiting. From the Gilded Zephyrs above, the course looks like water draining toward a single point. From the Rim-Tracker, it looks like three very different things all trying to occupy the same space.

Marble Wall is not viable. It has never intended to be viable. It has taken up position in the central approach canyon — the most direct route from any outer band to the Resonator — and it has *stopped moving*. The Bastion's crew has deployed its reactive armor skirts, broadening the vehicle's effective footprint to wall-to-wall canyon coverage. CD: 155. They cannot discharge. They are a gate.

### The Dancer & Fabian Problem — Expanded

Dancer & Fabian (CD −15) are routing for the Grand Resonator along the Trail Mark path they planted two phases ago. The dry sea-bed corridor is clear and familiar and exactly what their precision strategy called for. Until it terminates.

The central approach canyon exits into open ground thirty meters from the Resonator pylon. Marble Wall occupies the canyon exit. The Glass-Sail's resonant-phasing hull can compress through narrow gaps, but there is no narrow gap here — just thirty tons of reactive armor, stationary and patient.

**Fabian to Dancer:** *"We're not outrunning them. We need another door."*

They need a sync — but not for the CU. They've already cleared deficit. They need a new boon.

### The Dew Crystal Standoff — Expanded

Two rigs are converging on the Dew Crystal spire simultaneously. Sail & Stun (CD: 10) spotted it via True Sight and banked east through the hidden canyon route. Dancer & Fabian (CD: −15) need it for the Coolant Veil boon, not for the connection points. Two charges on the spire. Two teams approaching from different angles along the eastern wet shelf — the sound of two hulls threading the narrow water-carved rock, drawing toward the same point.

They arrive within fifteen seconds of each other. Both Harvest-Prongs are in range.

They face each other. A beat of silence on the Race Marshal's frequency. The Rim-Tracker operator doesn't know which way to pan.

Sail & Stun breaks right — they peel off and take the long canyon-wall route around the eastern shelf.

*Was this an agreement? Did Nyx, watching their timing from the Rim-Tracker west observation car, plan something in advance? There is no answer in the official record.*

> **GM Decision:** This moment can be left ambiguous for atmosphere, or resolved definitively: Nyx and Sail & Stun have a pre-existing professional relationship — she tracked their race patterns during pre-season trials and offered tactical advice. The yield was agreed in advance as part of an informal arrangement with no written record. If PCs confront Nyx about it later, she won't deny it: *"Sail & Stun needed a strong finish. Dancer needed to complete. Both outcomes were better than a contested crash."* Her interest was never in the flag. It was in the data.

**Dancer & Fabian take the Dew Crystal (25 CU).** CD: −15 → −40. BOON: **Coolant Veil** — the Glass-Sail's heat signature drops to near zero, and whatever engine hum it was producing dies entirely.

> **Spectator Note:** The Rim-Tracker stops dead at the east curve. Nyx, who has said nothing all race, uncaps her stopwatch. Clicks it. Nods once.

### Dancer & Fabian's Approach — Expanded

Marble Wall's crew is running its sensor arrays on the central approach lanes: heat detection and audio monitoring, the two broadest channels. Both are suppressed. The Glass-Sail's Coolant Veil masks its thermal signature. The Bastion's own Mute Wake makes its engine noise non-contributory to local audio — but doesn't give it hearing. Marble Wall has excellent silence and no additional sensory advantage.

Dancer & Fabian approach the canyon base from due east, hugging the wall. The corridor is unfrequented — it was never on any team's routing plan because it terminates in a sheer rock face fifty meters west of the canyon mouth. For most rigs, it's a dead end. For the Glass-Sail, it's a door.

The resonant-phasing property of the hull is not dramatic when it activates. There's a slight shimmer along the hull's contact surfaces, a low harmonic — more vibration than sound — and the material goes slightly translucent at the edges. Prow-first, the Glass-Sail compresses through a fracture in the canyon's eastern face, a crack three meters wide that no other vehicle in the field could use.

On the other side: the central canyon approach, behind the Bastion, between the Bastion and the Grand Resonator pylon.

Marble Wall's crew clocks the movement half a second too late. The sensor operator calls it. The captain calls to pivot. The Bastion begins to turn.

Heavy, slow. The reactive armor skirts drag. The hull is thirty tons plus deployed armor mass.

The Glass-Sail is already sprinting.

## Phase 6 — Loom-Pulse 3 and the Final Run

### Atmosphere

The third horn fires, and the crowd that has been building tension for sixty minutes releases it all at once. The Chime Spire erupts from the north flats — the most valuable node in the standard field, held back until the final Loom-Pulse to force late-race competition — its resonance signature registering on every instrument in the Scrivener booth simultaneously. The scoreboard updates. The north bleachers are on their feet. The north bleachers are twelve people.

The only rig within range of the Chime Spire is Pudge.

**Loom-Pulse 3 fires. The last wave of spires erupts.**

**North Open Flats (newly available):** Chime Spire (80 CU, 1 charge)

### Pudge's Final Sync — Expanded

Pudge is in the north flats at CD 70, the Gryphon-Hybrid riding the Loadlight thermal surge, half-mass and impossibly mobile. The Chime Spire erupts from the flat two hundred meters ahead, exactly on intercept.

The gryphon's dive on the Chime Spire is not graceful. It is aggressive and loud — the animal's cry bouncing across the north volcanic plain as it folds one wing and drops into an attack angle that drops the hull's Harvest-Prong directly onto the pylon.

**Pudge hits the Chime Spire (80 CU).** CD: 70 → **−10**. BOON: **Harmonic Lock** — one active boon stabilized for an additional pulse. Pudge immediately locks Loadlight active.

**CD ≤ 0. Pudge is legal for the Grand Resonator.**

The problem is geography. The Grand Resonator is in the exact center of the basin. Between the north flats and the center: a mile of mixed terrain, contested by teams that have been running south and west bands all race. Marble Wall's Mute Wake is active. The approach lanes are occupied.

Pudge doesn't route through.

The gryphon catches the Loadlight-extended thermal — Loadlight halves effective mass, which means the animal is lifting something that feels approximately like a large dog — and climbs. The Gryphon-Hybrid ascends at an angle that makes every aeronautics engineer in the Gilded Zephyrs airships above start doing mental calculations they don't like the outcome of.

The Gilded Zephyr observation crews are looking *down* at the basin. The Gryphon-Hybrid is looking up at them.

From the north rim, Professor Kante is watching through binoculars. They set them down.

> **Celia Vance** (running event logistics from the air) says something into her terminal. The cameras pivot north. The Gilded Zephyrs adjust altitude.

## Phase 7 — The Grand Resonator

### The Final Thirty Seconds — Atmosphere

Three rigs are converging on the Grand Resonator simultaneously, and none of them are approaching from the same direction. From above — from the Gilded Zephyrs, from the magnifying glass floors of Harmony's wealthiest citizens — the race looks like a diagram of vectors. Something is coming from the ground east. Something is coming from the ground west. Something is coming from directly above.

Marble Wall is between the ground approaches and the pylon. Its Harvest-Prong is deployed but its CD is 155 — too high for discharge, far too high to attempt the pylon. What it *can* do is prevent anyone smaller from getting close.

| Team | CD | Approach Route | Distance from Pylon |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Dancer & Fabian | −40 | Center approach, emerged behind Marble Wall | ~80 meters, gap closing |
| Sail & Stun | +10 | East canyon route, one sync needed | ~150 meters, found a Breathable Algae pocket |
| Pudge | −10 | Airborne, descending from the north | ~300 meters altitude, diving |

### The Convergence — Expanded

**Sail & Stun** finds the Breathable Algae pocket on the east canyon approach (20 CU). CD: 10 → **−10**. They are now legal. Still 150 meters from the pylon, running flat-out through the canyon mouth.

From behind Marble Wall, the Glass-Sail is 80 meters from the pylon. The Coolant Veil is still active. The Bastion is mid-pivot, its reactive armor skirts dragging on the canyon floor with the sound of metal on stone. Dancer is at the hull helm. Fabian has the Harvest-Prong arm deployed.

Marble Wall fires its ramming prow at the Glass-Sail's stern.

The hull resonant-phasing deflects the contact — but "deflects" is doing a lot of work. What actually happens is that the ramming prow *partly* passes through the stern geometry as it phases, and the remaining contact shunts the Glass-Sail sideways rather than stopping it. The impact is enough. The Glass-Sail spins off its approach vector, Fabian bracing against the hull cradle, Dancer fighting the rudder.

**Dancer & Fabian are off course.** They'll recover — but it costs them five seconds they didn't have to spend.

**The basin holds its breath.**

### Pudge's Descent — Expanded

Forty meters up. The Gryphon-Hybrid is a shape against the sky to every viewer on the basin floor — backlit, wings extended to control the dive angle, the gyro-boat hull swinging beneath it like a plumb line. The Harvest-Prong arm has been extended since the Chime Spire sync. The gryphon's eyes are locked on the pylon apex.

The Grand Resonator is a twelve-meter obsidian column. Nobody has ever discharged into it from above.

The race rulebook does not prohibit aerial approach.

**Sail & Stun** is 60 meters from the pylon, running flat.

**Pudge is fifteen meters up.**

The gryphon folds its wings.

The Harvest-Prong makes contact with the pylon's apex, the crown of the column, from directly above. Not from the side — from *above*. The discharge contact is optimal — the Prong seats against the resonance-active crystal at the spire's peak and the full accumulated CU dumps in a single pulse.

**The Grand Resonator ignites.**

A pillar of brilliant purple-white light erupts *upward* — not radially outward, not in the horizontal discharge rings that spectators are used to seeing from ground-level strikes. It goes straight up, a column sixty meters tall, coherent and blinding in the morning air. From the Gilded Zephyrs above it, the discharge registers on their instruments first and their eyes second.

The Gryphon-Hybrid is inside the column for two full seconds.

The gryphon screams — not in pain, in pure alarm — and banks sideways, clearing the column with its wings folded tight. Pudge is hanging on with both hands, laughing, the umber gutter box mounted on the hull's port side running dark as the stored connection empties completely. The hull swings wide on the rigging as the gryphon recovers into a banking circle above the basin.

Below the discharge column, the canvas of the race scoreboard updates.

**THE RACE IS CALLED.**

## Key Narrative Moments (Player Touchpoints)

> Use 2–3 of these during the race montage. Each one gives a PC a point of connection to the events.

### 1. The Missing Spire (Ignatius / Any Ash-Blood PC)

The Ash-Blood terrain slice is the northeastern corner of the basin — visually unmistakable to anyone who's seen images of the volcanic isles. Heat shimmer rising from scored rock. Flash-arc hazards sparking from the resonance-active substrate. The orange-and-black geography of Ignatius's home, reproduced in miniature for twenty thousand spectators to watch competitors drive through.

And no Spire.

The Scrivener calculations always produce a visible "ripple zone" at the surface where a pylon's underground housing sits — the earth moves slightly, a shimmer above the impact point where the hydraulic charge is loaded but not yet fired. Ignatius, if he knows what to look for, can see the ripple zone in the Ash-Blood sector. The slot is prepared. The pylon is not there.

*"The data isn't finalised."* — Every faculty member who is asked.

If Ignatius watches long enough: the ripple zone is present through all three Loom-Pulses. Nothing fires. The entire Ash-Blood terrain sector sees zero spire interaction across the full race.

### 2. The Gryphon's Physics (Iggy)

Every student who paid attention in Kante's class is watching Pudge's gryphon with different eyes than everyone else in the stands. The Loadlight boon halves effective mass — but effective mass is a registered measure of the rig's *powered mechanical systems*. The gryphon is biological. Organic. Its metabolism runs on ambient ether, not stored connection.

The umber gutter is calibrated to suppress the gryphon's contribution to the rig's CD calculation — but the gutter is a mechanical system, and the gryphon is not. The gryphon's ambient ether contribution is being *artificially suppressed by a calibration*. If that calibration fails, or if the gutter takes heat stress damage, the gryphon's metabolism partially overrides it.

Iggy, if watching through binoculars from a high bleacher position, has the information to form the question: *"If the gryphon is already outside the dead zone by design — just penalized — then what happens if the penalty system loosens?"*

This is the exact question Kante is asking from the north rim, with a notebook full of gryphon metabolic data going back three seasons.

### 3. The Battery Lesson (All)

When Shatter Stamper's Relay Ping fires and the channel sits open for eighteen seconds without a response, the crowd laughs. Nervous, uncertain laughter — the kind that comes from watching someone very confident fail publicly.

A 170 CD start. All that iron. All that crew. They never built an alliance. They accumulated CU the hard way, ran every major south-band node, and still couldn't reach the pylon before a gryphon bird-dove it from forty meters up.

The race proves Kante's thesis in two hours: *connection, not power.* A gyro-boat with a gryphon beat a dreadnought on treads.

### 4. Mizizi Terrain (Britt / Aggie)

The west petrified forest simulation is visually accurate. That's the point — the Circuit-Run is propaganda as much as sport, designed to teach Harmony's population what newly integrated territories look like. The Scrivener Guild produced the recreation from survey data: every column the right diameter, the fossilized canopy overhead properly dense, the stone bark texture of the trunks correctly patterned.

For Britt and Aggie, watching rigs race through a scaled simulation of their home, the moment may land in a way that's hard to articulate. Home, rendered as a racing obstacle. Home, rendered as a teaching aid for Harmony citizens who've never been there and likely never will be.

The terrain sector ends at the west canyon climb. The west canyon leads to the central approach. The central approach is where Marble Wall is sitting.

*Rill is in the maintenance pits on the basin floor, calibrating a battery. They haven't looked up at the forest simulation once.*

## Post-Race

### Aftermath (within the hour)

The discharge column dissipates in under a minute, but the light lingers as an afterimage across the basin. The Grand Resonator's crystal crown dims from active purple-white to a dark amber hold-charge. The scoreboard locks. The Scrivener Guild's booth finalizes the official timing stamps.

Pudge is pulled from the gryphon's back by their crew chief. The gryphon is circling overhead, loud, not ready to land — the animal runs on adrenaline the same as anything else. The crew chief is yelling Pudge's name and Pudge is laughing so hard they can't get their harness undone without help. Professor Kante is already descending from the North Research Deck with a clipboard and a set of questions that are going to take a while to ask.

Marble Wall is DQ'd. The post-discharge rule violation — they struck Dancer & Fabian's hull after the race was officially called — generates an immediate ejection from the certification zone. Their crew captain is still arguing when the marshals escort them out. He'll be arguing for weeks. Nobody official is listening.

Dancer & Fabian receive a standing ovation from the south bleachers — not because third place is particularly notable, but because *everyone saw* what happened. The Glass-Sail took a direct ram from The Bastion post-discharge, recovered course, and struck the pylon clean. The crowd doesn't always cheer for winners. Sometimes it cheers for the people who finished despite everything.

Lucky is already selling Dancer & Fabian colors.

The Global Amplitude display over the gate reads the same number it did at the start of the race. Every accumulated CU point has dissipated or discharged. None of it went to the network. The race produced no net gain for Harmony's power infrastructure. It never does. That's not what it's for.
