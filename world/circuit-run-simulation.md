## Starting Field

### Connection Deficit Formula

`CD = Global Amplitude (25) + Craft Draw + Launch Penalty − Stored Connection`

- **LED 1 green** when CD drops below `Global Amplitude` value (rig running clean)
- **LED 2 green** when CD ≤ 0 (discharge eligible)
- LED 2 cannot precede LED 1 — sequential by math

> **GA Note:** Global Amplitude is set at **25 CU** — below the standard 40–50 to avoid a long event at low network output. Not publicly disclosed. Spectators see the LEDs and Apex Compass only.

## Phase 0 — Certification Lock

### Atmosphere

The basin smells like hot metal and stone dust. Two hundred thousand people have been filing into the amphitheatres since before dawn. The south rim bleacher-walls vibrate — not from crowd noise but from the Loom-Pulse mechanisms cycling beneath, deep subterranean thrumming felt in teeth more than ears.

The **Apex Compass** rings the inner basin wall: sixteen arcs, all currently dark. Pre-race, it shows nothing — the Spires haven't erupted yet. The crowd studies the dark ring the way sailors study a horizon. Something is about to happen there.

The Walker-Core has positioned two legs on the southern rim. Its elevated frame towers thirty meters over the launch gantry, a four-legged mech silhouetted against the morning sky. From the south bleachers, it looks like it's deciding whether to step into the race itself.

At the Southern Gate below: land rigs queue west (canyon stone, heavy iron grilles, gear-oil smell), water rigs east (hulls in the shallows, sail geometry being adjusted, a different kind of noise entirely). Two sporting traditions at the same line.

**T-24 hours:** Officials seal declared draw bands and launch classes.

- **Controversy:** Pudge's Gryphon-Hybrid draw is disputed. Ruled as **Experimental class** — Heavy launch penalty offset by the light gyro-boat hull. CD: 195 — equal to Marble Wall on paper, for completely different reasons.
- **Calculus Prime** posts odds: *Shatter Stamper: 68.4%. Pudge: 3.1%. Dancer & Fabian: 0.8%.* The crowd laughs. Calculus Prime does not.
- **Lucky** is already working the bleachers in Shatter Stamper burgundy.

## Phase 1 — The Drop / Southern Rush

### Atmosphere

The opening horn fires in ascending chord — three brass instruments in sequence, each a fifth higher. By the third note the basin is already roaring.

At the Southern Gate, land rigs spill left through the canyon approach; water rigs angle right into the estuary channel. Two streams of metal diverging like a forked river.

Above: from the Walker-Core's gantry arm, the aerial rigs launch.

Then — **the Apex Compass flickers alive.**

Sixteen arcs across the basin rim shimmer and resolve. This is the moment the Scrivener Guild releases the first-wave node data: fully randomised, none of it known before right now. Teams that pre-drew precise routes are already recalculating. The crowd is reading the Compass like a map.

> **Which arcs glow and what color:** Roll or choose for your table. The south-east arc glows **silver** (Lenoa / Friction Needles). The south-west arc glows **earth-brown** (Feltland / Live Soil). Due south glows **dark charcoal** (Shepta / Whetstone). North remains dark. Northeast remains dark. East-southeast remains dark.
>
> None of the opening wave has landed in the north or in the two absent arc sectors. The north Apex Compass stays unlit. For now.

### Launch Sequence

| Order | Rig | Launch Style | Initial Trajectory |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1st | Stinger | Slung hard east from Walker-Core arm | Toward the silver Friction Needles arc |
| 2nd | Dancer & Fabian | Controlled sail arc from gantry | Wide parabola toward Whetstone shelf via estuary channel |

### The Drop — Expanded

Stinger goes sideways off the Walker-Core arm — the sling catches the hover-bike at a nearly horizontal angle, and the razor frame skips across the rim wind before correcting into a hard banking dive toward the eastern canyon mouth. The silver Lenoa arc on the Compass is steady southeast. Stinger read it in under two seconds and is already en route. Clear thinking under noise.

At the Southern Gate, the two streams separate cleanly. Marble Wall hits the land lane and almost immediately begins turning — not toward a Spire, the Compass tells everyone where that is — but across the field toward the east. That's not a node direction. That's a heading toward the hover-bike that just launched.

Then Pudge enters the air, and the crowd goes quiet.

The Walker-Core arm releases and the gryphon catches the thermal from the basin's north volcanic shelf — a 90-degree bank into ascending air, the hull swinging beneath it like a pendulum. The Apex Compass north arc is dark. No nodes there. No signal. Everyone with a Compass reading is watching the same dark arc and watching the Gryphon-Hybrid head directly toward it.

Someone in the Bleacher-Walls shouts *"WHAT IS THAT IDIOT DOING?!"*

The crowd exhales into laughter, tension releasing all at once. Calculus Prime, in the high bleachers, writes something in their ledger.

> **GM Beat:** Pudge banking north should generate player reactions — Iggy might lean forward, because heading toward a dark Compass arc is either lunacy or a plan for something the Compass doesn't show yet. Kante, watching from the North Research Deck, is already smiling.

The **orange flag** rises over the western sector. The Stinger Compass row on the scoreboard goes dark.

## Phase 5 — The Convergence

### Atmosphere

Two teams are discharge-eligible. The Compass shows where the remaining nodes are — mid-basin arcs still dim and muted, east shelf still active. Marble Wall is somewhere in the mid-basin with its engines silent, both LEDs red, moving toward the north flats where the Chime spire hasn't appeared yet.

The east band carries a quiet race: Dancer & Fabian versus Sail & Stun converging toward the ice-blue Dew Crystal arc from different angles.

### The Dew Crystal Standoff

Two charges on the Prima Dew Crystal spire — ice blue, condensation running down it constantly, a small puddle forming at the base from runoff. Two rigs approaching from different angles.

The Compass arc pulses for both teams simultaneously. Both activation windows open.

Sail & Stun yields. They break right and take the long canyon-wall arc around the eastern shelf.

Was this agreed in advance? No official record exists.

> **GM Decision:** Nyx and Sail & Stun have a pre-existing professional relationship. The yield was informal, no written record. If PCs ask Nyx later: *"Sail & Stun needed a strong finish. Dancer needed the boon position. Both outcomes were better than a crash."* She means it.

**Dancer & Fabian — Dew Crystal (25 CU, sole sync, no contention).** CD: −15 → **−40**. Boon: **Coolant Veil** — heat and noise signature drops to near zero.

> *Had Sail & Stun stayed: both teams earn 25 + 10 = 35 CU. Dancer's CD was already negative, so the bonus is nice but not essential. Sail & Stun gave up 10 CU to give Dancer the Coolant Veil uncontested. That's the trade.*

### The Glass-Sail's Approach

Marble Wall has occupied the central approach lane at last — but its Mute Wake keeps it silent, and it has no thermal lock either. The Bastion is running audio and heat detection. Coolant Veil kills Dancer's thermal profile. Mute Wake kills Marble Wall's own audio contribution to the basin.

Dancer & Fabian route east to the canyon base — looking for the fracture they flagged during Certification Lock terrain surveys three nights ago. A three-meter crack in the canyon's eastern face, noted in their pre-race notes as a phase-through candidate.

The hull resonant-phasing activates: slight shimmer at the contact surfaces, a low harmonic, material going slightly translucent at the edges. Prow-first, the Glass-Sail compresses through.

On the other side: the central approach, **behind Marble Wall, between The Bastion and the Grand Resonator pylon**.

The Bastion's sensor operator clocks the movement half a second too late. Heavy, slow — the reactive armor skirts drag. Dancer & Fabian are already sprinting.

> **Post-race analysis:** In a standard-length race with Global Amplitude of 40–50 CU, Marble Wall would have had more pulses. This compressed race punished late-starters disproportionately. The strategy is valid. This race was the wrong size for it.

### Final Approach

| Team | CD | Approach | Distance |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Dancer & Fabian | −40 | Central approach, behind Marble Wall | ~80m |
| Sail & Stun | 10 | East canyon — found Breathable Algae pocket | ~150m |

### The Final Thirty Seconds

Marble Wall fires its ramming prow at the Glass-Sail's stern — a last attempt to stop the team they should have targeted in Phase 3. The resonant-phasing deflects partial contact, shunting the Glass-Sail sideways. Five seconds lost.

Five seconds is exactly how long it takes Pudge to close the remaining altitude.

Forty meters above the basin floor. The Gryphon-Hybrid is a shape against the sky — backlit, wings extended, hull swinging beneath like a plumb line. The Harvest-Prong arm extended since the Chime Spire sync. The gryphon's eyes locked on the pylon apex.

The rulebook does not prohibit aerial approach.

The gryphon folds its wings.

Sail & Stun is 60 meters from the pylon. Still sprinting.

**Pudge is fifteen meters up.**

The Harvest-Prong makes contact with the pylon's crown — from directly above, not from the side — and the full accumulated connection discharges in a single pulse.

**The Grand Resonator ignites.**

A pillar of brilliant purple-white light erupts *upward* — not radially, not the horizontal rings spectators expected. Straight up. Sixty meters, coherent and blinding. The Gilded Zephyrs above register it on instruments before eyes.

The gryphon is inside the column for two full seconds. It screams — pure alarm — and banks sideways, clearing the column with wings folded tight. Pudge hanging on with both hands, laughing, umber gutter running dark as everything empties.

**THE RACE IS CALLED.**

## Player Touchpoints (Use 2–3 per session)

### 1. The Missing Ash-Blood Spire (Ignatius)
The Apex Compass north-northeast arc is dark from the opening horn to the final call. Not dim — dark. The underground housing for that spire exists; its ripple zone is visible in the soil for anyone who knows what to look for. Ignatius does.

### 2. The Gryphon's Physics (Iggy)
