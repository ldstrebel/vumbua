# The First Vumbua Circuit-Run: Full Race Simulation

> **GM Reference** — This is the complete play-by-play for the opening [[circuit-run|Circuit-Run]] at [[Vumbua Academy]]. Use it to run the race as a vivid spectacle, or adapt it scene-by-scene based on player interaction. The session-facing overview lives in [[sessions/planning/Session 4 plan|Session 4 Plan]].

## Starting Field

### Connection Deficit at the Drop

`CD = Global Amplitude (25) + Craft Draw + Launch Penalty`

> **Note:** The Global Amplitude is set at **25 CU** for this race — reflecting Harmony's anomalously low network output. Race officials quietly lowered the threshold from the usual 40–50 to avoid an embarrassingly long event. This is not publicly disclosed.
>
> **GM Presentation:** The spectators and PCs see the Global Amplitude display on the gate scoreboard but do not see the base threshold value — they only see accumulated CU totals for active rigs. The "quieter race" can pass as normal variation. If a PC who understands the system (Iggy, post-Kante conversations) asks a marshal why the race finished so quickly, a deflective answer (*"Good field this year"*) is appropriate. The truth about the lowered threshold is an optional reveal for a later conversation with Kante.

## Phase 0 — Certification Lock

**T-24 hours:** Officials seal declared draw bands and launch classes.

- **Controversy:** Pudge's Gryphon-Hybrid draw is disputed. The gyro-boat itself rates as Light class, but the gryphon's metabolic drive adds effective mass. Officials rule it enters as **Experimental class**, with a Heavy launch penalty offset by the light hull. Final CD lands at 195 — equal to Marble Wall on paper, but for completely different reasons.
- **Calculus Prime** (Squad 04) projects final odds from the high bleachers: *"Shatter Stamper, 68.4%. Pudge, 3.1%. Dancer & Fabian, 0.8% — rounding error."* The crowd around them laughs. Calculus Prime does not.
- **Lucky** is already circulating in the bleachers, selling *Shatter Stamper* flags and taking bets. He is working very hard. He always is.

**Opening Horn fires. The basin erupts.**

The rigs are slung or dropped from the Southern Rim in weight-class order, lightest first.

### Marble Wall's First Hunt

The Bastion doesn't race for nodes. It tracks Stinger.

The hover-bike tries to use **Slipline** to sprint clear before Marble Wall closes — but Whetstone's charges are both gone. There's no second node left in the south band. Stinger is fast, but not fast enough to outrun the Bastion in a straight canyon. Marble Wall shadows them, not striking yet. Waiting.

> **Spectator Reaction:** The Bleacher-Walls go strangely quiet as the Bastion's bulk enters the canyon behind the razor hover-bike. Everyone senses what's coming.

### The Marble Wall vs Stinger Confrontation

Stinger is routing for the center. The petrified forest offers less speed advantage — the trees are tight, and the hover-bike's slipstream is disrupted by the stone pillars.

Marble Wall follows them in. The Bastion's forward ram charges.

Stinger sees it coming. Hard dodge left — but the Bastion fires its kinetic dampeners sideways, creating a sudden gravity pulse. The hover-bike's lift mechanism stutters. The razor frame cartwheels into a petrified trunk.

**STINGER IS DISABLED.** Crash webbing deploys. Flare dye blooms orange across the stone bark. The pilot is shaken, battered, alive.

> **Race Marshal Status:** Orange flag raised over the western sector. Two minutes later, a retrieval team rope-slides from the rim. The race continues.

**Marble Wall pivots toward the Grand Resonator approach lane.** CD: 175. They can't discharge yet — but they don't need to. They're not here to win. They're here to make sure no one else does either.

> **GM Beat:** If Cassius Thorne is visible in the bleachers, he's shouting "YES! THAT'S STRATEGY!" Iron-Jaw Jax is shaking the railing. Lucky is frantically swapping his flags.

## Phase 5 — The Convergence

### The Marble Wall Checkpoint

Marble Wall has taken up position in the central approach canyon — the most direct route from any outer band to the Grand Resonator. CD: 155. They cannot discharge yet, but they can stop everyone else.

The configuration is clear: **The Bastion is a gate.** Any rig routing through the central canyon will meet thirty tons of reactive armor with no room to pass.

### The Dancer & Fabian Problem

Dancer & Fabian (CD −15) trail back toward the Grand Resonator using their planted Trail Mark path through the east-center dry sea-bed. The path is clear — until it terminates in the same central approach canyon where Marble Wall waits.

The Glass-Sail's resonant-phasing hull property lets it compress through narrow gaps. But "narrow gap" and "Marble Wall's position" are mutually exclusive.

**Fabian to Dancer:** *"We're not outrunning them. We need another door."*

They need a new sync — but for the boon, not the CU. They've already beaten their CD.

### The Dew Crystal Standoff

Sail & Stun (CD: 10) and Dancer & Fabian (CD: −15) are both converging on the Dew Crystal spire in the eastern wet shelf. **Only two charges. Two rigs approaching.**

Both teams arrive nearly simultaneously. **They face each other.** A beat of silence on the Race Marshal's frequency.

Sail & Stun breaks right — they peel off and take the long route around the canyon wall.

*Was this an agreement? Did Nyx, watching their timing from the Rim-Tracker on the west curve, plan something in advance? There is no answer on the official record.*

> **GM Decision:** This moment can be left ambiguous for atmosphere, or resolved definitively: Nyx and Sail & Stun have a pre-existing professional relationship — she tracked their race patterns during pre-season trials and offered tactical advice. The yield was agreed in advance as part of an informal arrangement with no written record. If PCs confront Nyx about it, she won't deny it: *"Sail & Stun needed a strong finish. Dancer needed to complete. Both outcomes were better than a contested crash."* Her interest was never in the flag. It was in the data.

**Dancer & Fabian take the Dew Crystal (25 CU).** CD: −15 → −40. BOON: **Coolant Veil** (reduce heat/noise signatures). The Glass-Sail becomes thermally and acoustically invisible.

> **Spectator Note:** The Rim-Tracker stops dead at the east curve. Nyx, who has said nothing all race, uncaps her stopwatch. Clicks it. Nods once.

### Dancer & Fabian's Approach

The Coolant Veil masks the Glass-Sail from Marble Wall's sensors. Their Mute Wake renders the Bastion silent — but silent doesn't mean blind.

Dancer & Fabian approach the canyon base from the east. Marble Wall's crew is scanning heat and audio channels — both suppressed. The Glass-Sail reaches the canyon mouth undetected.

The hull resonant-phases through a micro-gap in the canyon wall's eastern face — a fracture too narrow for any other rig but just wide enough for the Glass-Sail's compressed form.

**The Glass-Sail emerges behind the Bastion, between the Bastion and the Grand Resonator.**

Marble Wall's crew realizes the error a half-second later. The Bastion begins to pivot — heavy, slow. The Glass-Sail is already sprinting for the pylon.

**But then Pudge enters the picture.**

## Phase 6 — Loom-Pulse 3 and the Final Run

**Loom-Pulse 3 fires. The last wave of spires erupts.**

**North Open Flats (newly available):** Chime Spire (80 CU, 1 charge)

Pudge is still in the north flats, currently at CD 70, the Gryphon-Hybrid riding the Loadlight thermal surge from the Lift Stone sync. The Chime Spire erupts from the flat, 200 meters ahead.

**Pudge hits the Chime Spire (80 CU).** CD: 70 → **−10**. BOON: **Harmonic Lock** (stabilize one active boon for +1 pulse). Pudge immediately uses it to lock Loadlight active for one additional pulse.

**CD ≤ 0. Pudge is legal for the Grand Resonator.**

The Gryphon-Hybrid is in the north. The Grand Resonator is in the center. Between them: a mile of open terrain, contested by teams that have been running the south and west bands all race.

Pudge doesn't run through that terrain. The gryphon banks hard, rising on the Loadlight-extended thermal. **The climb is absurd.** The gryphon-hybrid is going UP.

From the Gilded Zephyrs, the luxury airships' magnifying floors turn to face the north end of the basin. Celia Vance, running event logistics from the air, says something into her terminal. The cameras pivot.

## Phase 7 — The Grand Resonator

### The Final Thirty Seconds

Three rigs are converging on the Grand Resonator simultaneously:

| Team | CD | Approach Route | Distance from Pylon |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Dancer & Fabian | −40 | Center approach, emerged behind Marble Wall | ~80 meters, gap closing |
| Sail & Stun | +10 | East canyon route, one sync needed | ~150 meters, found a Breathable Algae pocket |
| Pudge | −10 | Airborne, descending from the north | ~300 meters altitude, diving |

Marble Wall (CD: 155, cannot discharge) is trying to intercept Dancer & Fabian from behind. The Bastion's pivot is sluggish — reactive armor is heavy.

**Sail & Stun** finds the Breathable Algae pocket on the east canyon approach (20 CU). CD: 10 → **−10**. They are now legal. Still 150 meters from the pylon.

**The Convergence:**

1. Dancer & Fabian are 80 meters out, hull phasing slightly as they push their speed limit. Marble Wall is 30 meters behind them, closing.
2. Sail & Stun rounds the eastern approach arc, 150 meters and falling.
3. Pudge's gryphon breaks the rim of the basin's interior airspace. The Gryphon-Hybrid is **airborne over the central basin**. Below: the Grand Resonator pylon.

Marble Wall fires its ramming prow at the Glass-Sail's stern.

**Dancer & Fabian snap the hull into a Kinetic-Echo boon** — wait, they used Coolant Veil last. The Kinetic-Echo boon is gone. But the Glass-Sail's own resonant-phasing lets the stern warp slightly, deflecting the ram. The impact shunts them sideways rather than stopping them.

**Dancer & Fabian spin out — they've lost their approach vector.** The Glass-Sail careens off course, Fabian fighting the rudder. They'll recover, but it costs them five critical seconds.

**The basin holds its breath.**

Pudge is forty meters up and falling. The Gryphon-Hybrid points its Harvest-Prong downward. The gryphon folds its wings into a dive.

**Sail & Stun is 60 meters out and running flat.**

**Pudge is FIFTEEN meters up.**

**Pudge dives onto the Grand Resonator from above.**

The Harvest-Prong strikes the pylon apex. Not from the side — from **above**. The discharge angle is perfect; full CU release into the spire's crown.

**The Grand Resonator ignites.**

A pillar of brilliant purple-white light erupts upward — not horizontally outward. The Gryphon-Hybrid is inside the discharge column for two full seconds before the gryphon banks sideways, shrieking. Pudge is holding on with both hands, laughing, the umber gutter going black from a full dump.

## Key Narrative Moments (Player Touchpoints)

> Use 2–3 of these during the race montage. Each one gives a PC a point of connection to the events.

### 1. The Missing Spire (Ignatius / Any Ash-Blood PC)
When the Ash-Blood terrain slice is visible from the bleachers — heat shimmer vents, flash-arc hazards, scorched rock — there is no Spire. The slot is simply... empty.

If Ignatius looks long enough, he sees where the Spire should be. The Scrivener calculations always produce a visible "ripple zone" where the pylon would erupt. The ripple zone is there. The pylon is not.

*"The data isn't finalised." — Every faculty member who is asked.*

### 2. The Gryphon's Physics (Iggy)
Every student who paid attention in Kante's class is watching Pudge's gryphon with new eyes. The Loadlight boon halves the rig's effective mass — but the gryphon's own mass is organic, not rated. Technically the gryphon itself is running on biological metabolism, not stored connection.

Iggy, if watching through binoculars, has the information to ask: *"If the gryphon is outside the umber gutter's dead zone... does it add back ambient ether that the gutter is trying to suppress?"* This is the exact question Kante is asking from the north rim.

### 3. The Battery Lesson (All)
When Shatter Stamper's Relay Ping fires and no one responds, the crowd actually laughs. A 170 CD start, all that iron, and they couldn't build an alliance. They accumulated CU the hard way and still couldn't reach the pylon in time.

The race proves Kante's lesson in two hours: *connection, not power*. A gyro-boat with a gryphon beat a dreadnought on treads.

### 4. Mizizi Terrain (Britt / Aggie)
The west petrified forest simulation is visually accurate to what the Mizizi territory actually looks like. If either PC sees it — the petrified spires, the stone bark texture, the bone-colored light through fossilized canopy — it might hit unexpectedly hard. Home, rendered as spectacle.

*Rill is on the basin floor in the maintenance pits, calibrating a battery. They haven't looked up at the forest once.*

### 5. Lucky's Running Commentary
Lucky is changing flags approximately every three minutes throughout the race. At some point, he sells a *Shatter Stamper* flag to someone, then three minutes later sells them a *Dancer & Fabian* flag at "favorable swap odds." If a PC bought a flag from him, he will absolutely try to upsell them on the "upgrade" after Marble Wall takes out Stinger.

### 6. Cinder-4's Vigil
If anyone with binoculars sweeps the west bleachers, they will find Cinder-4 staring at the empty Ash-Blood Spire slot. The construct's optical arrays are fixed on that exact location. It has not moved in forty minutes. A construct visibly in emotional distress is unsettling in a specific way.

## Post-Race

### Aftermath (within the hour)

- Pudge is pulled from the gryphon's back by their crew. They are immediately swarmed by people. Professor Kante descends from the research deck with a clipboard and several urgent questions.
- Marble Wall is DQ'd and ejected from the post-race certification zone. Their crew captain is still shouting.
- Dancer & Fabian receive a standing ovation from the South Bleachers. Someone in the crowd starts a chant. *Lucky is already selling their colors.*
- The Global Amplitude display over the gate reads the same number it did at the start. The race generated a lot of stored connection. None of it went to the network.

### Hooks

- **Professor Kante** wants to speak to anyone who noticed the gryphon anomaly. Iggy specifically.
- **Cinder-4** is now very still. Still staring at the empty slot. This does not resolve.
- **The post-race analysis** done by the Scrivener Guild will quietly note that the race's Ash-Blood terrain sector was the only section of the arena that saw zero spire interaction all race. The announcement of this statistic — that the newest territory produced *nothing* — generates a news cycle.
- **Rill** surfaces briefly at the edge of the pits, watching the post-race crowd. They catch Britt's eye if she's nearby. Then they're gone.
