## Phase 0 — Certification Lock

### Atmosphere

The basin smells like hot metal and stone dust. The Apex Compass rings the inner wall — sixteen arcs in total darkness. Pre-race, it shows nothing; the Spires haven't erupted yet. The crowd studies the dark ring like sailors reading a horizon.

The Walker-Core straddles the southern rim: four legs on stone, the drop arm curled over the basin interior like a scorpion tail. At the Southern Gate below, land rigs queue west (wide stone apron, gear-oil smell), water rigs east (hulls riding the estuary shallows, sail geometry being adjusted by hand).

**T-24 hours:** Officials seal all Craft Draw declarations and launch classes.

- **Controversy:** Pudge's Gryphon-Hybrid draw is disputed. Ruled as **Experimental class** — Heavy launch penalty offset by the light gyro-boat hull. Final CD: 195 — equal to Marble Wall, for entirely different reasons. The Harvest-Prong is rigged to Pudge's personal harness, not the hull cradle; the intent is an arm's-length aerial strike, and the Guild inspector raises an eyebrow but finds no rule against it.
- **Calculus Prime** posts odds: *Shatter Stamper: 68.4%. Pudge: 3.1%. Dancer & Fabian: 0.8%.* The crowd laughs. Calculus Prime does not.
- **Lucky** is already working the bleachers in Shatter Stamper burgundy, three flag-changes folded in his coat.

## Horn 1 — Opening / The Drop

### Atmosphere

The opening horn fires in an ascending chord — three brass instruments in sequence, each a fifth higher. By the third note the basin is roaring.

**The Apex Compass ignites.**

All sixteen arcs illuminate simultaneously as the first Spire wave breaches the surface — fully random, no team knew their positions in advance. The crowd reads the ring. Teams already moving adjust routes in real time. The pre-planned approaches of three mid-field crews become immediately wrong.

> **Which arcs light up (GM choice or roll):** South arc — **dark charcoal** (Shepta / Whetstone, canyon zone). South-southwest — **earth brown** (Feltland / Live Soil, forest zone). East-southeast — **silver** (Lenoa / Friction Needles, canyon). Far east — **ice blue** in the water zone (Prima / Dew Crystal, estuary). Deep south estuary — **bioluminescent green** (Bay of Breath, water floor). North: dark. Ash-Blood arc: dark.

At the Southern Gate both streams break simultaneously — land rigs spill west into the canyon approach, water rigs angle east into the estuary channel. Two sporting traditions separating within seconds.

The Walker-Core arm releases the aerial rigs:

| Order | Rig | Launch |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | Stinger | Slung east — hard bank toward the silver Friction Needles arc |
| 2nd | Dancer & Fabian | Wide sail arc — reading the forest arcs as they descend |
| Last | Pudge | Thermal catch off Walker-Core — banks NORTH immediately. The Compass north arc is dark. Nobody knows what Pudge is doing. |

### The Drop — Expanded

Stinger goes sideways off the drop arm. The sling catches the hover-bike at nearly horizontal angle, and the razor frame skips across rim wind before correcting into a hard diving bank toward the east canyon. The silver arc is southeast. Stinger has already calculated the approach; the Prong is attached to the pilot's left wrist via a locking brace — it won't need to dismount for a canyon strike if the geometry works.

At the gate, Marble Wall hits the land lane and almost immediately begins turning east. Not toward any lit Compass arc. Toward the same direction Stinger just flew.

Then Pudge enters the air, and the crowd goes quiet.

The thermal from the northern volcanic flats is reliable in the morning hours — Pudge has studied this. The gryphon catches it mid-bank and *climbs*. The hull swings beneath. The north Compass arc is completely dark. No Spires there. No signal. Pudge is flying toward nothing the race can currently offer.

*"WHAT IS THAT IDIOT DOING?!"*

The crowd exhales into laughter. Kante, on the North Research Deck, is already smiling.

> **GM Beat:** Iggy or anyone with knowledge of the north flats understands: there's nothing there now. But node geography means the Major nodes — Gilded, Juxta — always spawn in volcanic open terrain. Pudge is pre-positioning for Horn 2.

## Between Horn 1 and Horn 2 (~T+0 to T+40)

### Atmosphere

The south canyon and estuary zones are operating simultaneously and mostly separately. Land rigs navigate stone corridors; water rigs navigate the shallows and open channel. The Compass tracks them differently — canyon arcs and water arcs telling opposite stories.

**Drainage is ongoing for all rigs from Horn 1 forward.**

### South Canyon Spires — First Contact

Three arcs active in the south zone. At Close range the stone Spires reveal themselves:

- **Whetstone (Shepta):** Dark charcoal, extremely sharp geometric facets. It sparks where the resonance bleeds from the crown — visible in dim canyon light before you're within range.
- **Friction Needles (Lenoa):** Multiple thin silver needle-columns in a tight cluster rather than one pillar. Frictionless surface — the Prong needs precise application or it slides. The kind of node where dismounting and using two hands is common.
- **Live Soil (Feltland):** Earth-brown, vines at the base, persistently warm. It smells like something — actual soil, actual growth — in a stone canyon. Dissonant enough to slow people down for a moment before they sync.

### Water Zone Spires — First Contact

Two arcs active in the estuary. Dew Crystal is in the transition zone (shallow water, ankle-deep); Bay of Breath is in deeper water on the basin floor.

- **A water-rig striker can sync Dew Crystal without leaving the hull** — shallow enough to reach over the side with the Prong. Bay of Breath requires either a swimmer or a rig capable of dipping its Prong-arm underwater. Sail & Stun carries a designated swimmer in the crew; it's in the race plan.

## Horn 2 — Loom-Pulse 2 (~T+40)

### Atmosphere

The second horn fires. The Compass responds within seconds as the new wave erupts — this time with reactive logic, skewing toward the quietest arcs.

**Whetstone and Live Soil arcs went dark** (depleted between Horn 1 and 2). Both had at least one unsynced charge sitting idle — no companion spawn triggered there because the first charges *were* synced; it's the second charges that sat. By the rule, a companion spawns only if the node went **completely** untouched. These didn't qualify.

**New arcs illuminating (reactive wave):** North — **deep violet** (Umbra Mountain / Umber Crystal, open flats). North — **blue-grey** (Juxta / Lift Stone, volcanic terrain). West-forest — **bronze-amber** (Relina / Soft Forge). West-forest — **warm amber** (Minnow / Living Scent Wood). Mid-basin — **pale grey** (Octo / Speaking Stone). Mid-basin — **matte grey-brown** (Nstyl / Silent Silt).

Pudge, already in the north flats, is the only rig within range of both north arcs from the moment they appear.

**Companion spawn check (Horn 1 → Horn 2):** The Friction Needles node — second charge unsynced since eruption. A generic companion node erupts next to it in the east canyon. Grey-white, no city identity, no boon. **+18 CU** raw, two charges. The Compass silver arc splits into two close indicators. Anyone still in the east canyon has a bonus target.

## Between Horn 2 and Horn 3 (~T+40 to T+80)

### Atmosphere

Three active arcs remain from Horn 2. The Compass shows: Umber Crystal still has charges (deep violet, north), mid-basin arcs are depleting, the water zone is quieting. Dancers are eligible and routing toward the Grand Resonator — but the Convergence Horn hasn't fired yet. Marble Wall is silent and fast.

### Strategic Landscape

Dancer & Fabian need to reach the Grand Resonator pylon — but approaching it openly means crossing Marble Wall's line of sight. The Mute Wake makes The Bastion silent. The Glass-Sail doesn't know where it is.

At Certification Lock, three days before the race, Nyx took a terrain survey of the east canyon and logged a fracture in the eastern face: a three-meter crack in the stone, flagged as a potential phase-through candidate. The Glass-Sail has resonant-phasing capability. It's in the race plan.

**Sail & Stun** routes toward the estuary — looking for the last minor water nodes before mid-basin.

**Pudge** returns to the Umber Crystal (second charge) while the north arc is still available.

Dancer & Fabian route east, hug the canyon wall, and find the fracture. Three meters wide, flagged in Nyx's notes. The hull resonant-phasing activates — slight shimmer at the contact surfaces, material going slightly translucent at the edges. Prow-first, the Glass-Sail compresses through.

On the other side: the central approach lane, **behind Marble Wall, between The Bastion and the Grand Resonator.**

The Bastion's sensor operator catches the movement half a second too late.

## Horn 3 — Loom-Pulse 3 / Convergence Horn (~T+80)

### Atmosphere

Two horns fire in rapid sequence at T+80 — the third Loom-Pulse horn immediately followed by the Convergence Horn, because Dancer & Fabian's eligibility status has been live since Horn 2. Race officials were waiting for confirmation before sounding the Convergence call; Pudge going green on the approach to Horn 3 resolves any ambiguity.

The Compass: one new arc ignites bright gold in the north. The Chime Spire from Gilded — organ-pipe brass columns erupting from the volcanic flat, actual musical tones audible across the full basin as it breaches the surface. The most valuable node in the race. The only rig within range is Pudge.

**Then the second, longer horn. The Convergence Horn.**

The crowd recognises the different tone. The energy in the bleachers shifts — from race-watching to *outcome-waiting*.

**After the Convergence Horn: no new Spire waves. No new companion spawns. The Compass shows only what remains active.**

### Pudge Hits the Chime Spire

The gold arc pulses as the Gryphon-Hybrid descends into range. The organ-pipe columns ring — a deepening chord as the resonance registers the approach. Pudge leans forward from the harness, Prong extended arm's-length. Contact with the crown.

**Chime Spire (80 CU).** CD: −26 → **−106** (already negative; stored connection surging past double the GA threshold). Boon: **Harmonic Lock** — Loadlight effect stabilises.

North Compass arc goes dark behind Pudge as the Chime's charges deplete. The gold light is gone.

### Marble Wall's Final Run

The Bastion has been moving mid-basin all race. After the Convergence Horn: no more node waves. The Compass shows the remaining active arcs — a handful of minor nodes in the mid-basin and estuary. Marble Wall rushes to Clockwork Blooms (Bloomfield, +20 CU, west-to-mid).

**Marble Wall: 118 → 98.** The copper Compass arc pulses as The Bastion's striker dismounts and approaches on foot — the mechanical bloom-flowers opening and closing indifferently while they reach for the Prong contact point.

Drain: +13 per pulse. Two pulses remain in the Convergence window before drain overtakes any remaining node value. The math is closing. Marble Wall needs ~98 more CU and is running out of arcs.

**The aggro strategy required a faster first half.** Targeting Stinger (second-fastest threat) while Dancer & Fabian (the actual fastest threat) farmed freely was the error. In a standard race with Global Amplitude 40–50, there would have been more buffer. This race was too compressed.

Marble Wall cannot reach eligibility before the Grand Resonator is struck. The drain handles the rest.

## The Grand Resonator — Final Approach

### In the Air

Pudge at altitude: CD −106, both LEDs green, Chime boon active. The Gryphon-Hybrid is a silhouette against the open sky above the centre of the basin. Harvest-Prong arm still extended from the harness — the same position it's been in since Chime. The gryphon's eyes are locked on the pylon apex.

**Forty meters up. Thirty. Twenty.**

Marble Wall fires its ramming prow at the Glass-Sail's stern as Dancer & Fabian clear the approach lane — a last attempt at stopping the team they should have eliminated three horn intervals ago. The resonant-phasing deflects partial contact. The Glass-Sail spins sideways off its approach vector. **Five seconds lost.**

Five seconds is exactly how long it takes Pudge to close the remaining altitude.

**Sail & Stun** finds the last Breathable Algae node in the estuary shallows — the swimmer over the side, Prong dipped to the water floor, +20 CU. CD: 5 → **−15.** 🟢🟢 Both green. Third rig eligible. Sprinting for the pylon at 60 meters out.

Pudge is at fifteen meters.

**The Harvest-Prong contacts the Grand Resonator pylon crown — from directly above.**

The full stored connection discharges in a single pulse. Not radially outward. **Up.** A pillar of brilliant purple-white light, sixty meters coherent and vertical. The Gilded Zephyrs above register it on instruments before eyes.

The gryphon is inside the column for two full seconds. It screams — pure alarm — and banks sideways out of the column with wings folded, clearing by three meters. Pudge is hanging on with both hands, laughing, the umber gutter running completely dark.

**THE RACE IS CALLED.**

## Player Touchpoints

### 1. The Missing Ash-Blood Arc (Ignatius)
The Ash-Blood Compass arc is dark from Horn 1 to the final call. The terrain is there — accurately rendered, visible from the stands. No Spire rises from it across the entire race. The underground housing exists; Ignatius can see the resonance ripple if he knows where to look.

### 2. The Gryphon's Physics (Iggy)
Loadlight boon halved Pudge's registered mass. The gryphon's organic metabolism sits outside the umber gutter's calibration zone — it contributes ambient drive rather than stored connection. If the gutter takes structural damage mid-race, the organic component partially overrides the deficit suppression. Kante has been watching this failure mode for two seasons. Iggy has enough background to ask the right question.

### 3. The Scoreboard Silence
When the Convergence Horn eliminates Shatter Stamper — both LEDs still red, Relay Ping unanswered, 68.4% pre-race favourite — the east bleachers in burgundy go completely quiet. No jeering. Just watching the scoreboard show the same two red dots it showed at Horn 1.

### 4. Mizizi Terrain (Britt / Aggie)
The Live Soil spire from Feltland. The Living Scent Wood node from Minnow. Both standing in the petrified forest zone — warm, organic, faintly familiar. Cities nobody watching has likely ever visited, represented in stone and crystal in a sporting arena. *Rill is in the maintenance pits. They haven't looked at the forest zone once.*

### 5. Lucky's Flag Arc
Pre-Drop: Shatter Stamper (burgundy). After Stinger goes down: still Stamper, frantically. After Pudge hits Umber Crystal and LED 1 goes green: a Pudge flag appears from somewhere in his coat. After the Convergence Horn eliminates Stamper: a pre-printed Dancer & Fabian flag, ready. He had it ready.

### 6. Cinder-4's Vigil
West bleachers, same position, Horn 1 to final call. Optical arrays fixed on the Ash-Blood Compass arc the entire race. A construct that can track forty variables simultaneously, watching one dark arc for eighty minutes.

A Kiln teammate puts a hand on Cinder-4's shoulder after the race ends. No response. This doesn't resolve today.

### 7. Rill's Non-Watch
Rill surfaces at the maintenance pit edge during post-race dispersal. Catches Britt's eye — one beat — then disappears into the service corridor. They were never watching the forest simulation. They were watching to see who was.
