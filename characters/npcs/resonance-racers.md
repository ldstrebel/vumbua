# Resonance Racers

This page details the notable teams and soloists competing in the intake year's opening [[circuit-run|Resonance Race]].

## Team 1: Shatter Stamper
| | |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Heavy-Class Racing Team |
| **Rig** | **The Ironclad** — A massive, brutalist heavy rig built for straight-line speed and surviving impacts. Its hull is reinforced with layered iron plate and runs on an overclocked drive system that generates impressive raw momentum. What it lacks in maneuverability it compensates for with sheer mass and engine output. |
| **Crew** | 3-person: pilot, harpooner, engineer |
| **Starting CD** | 170 |
| **Strategy** | **Brute Force & Node-Farming.** Routes directly for high-yield nodes to offset their enormous craft draw. The Ironclad is built to take punishment, not avoid it — they pick the contested nodes and dare smaller rigs to fight them for it. |
| **Weakness** | No pre-race alliances; their Relay Ping boon has no one to relay to. Their high CD means they're always behind on timing. |
| **Notes** | Calculus Prime gives them 68.4% win odds pre-race. The crowd believes it. The race does not. |

## Team 2: Sail & Stun
| | |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Speed/Disruption Class |
| **Rig** | **The Stinger-Ketch** — A fast sailing vessel rigged for competitive open-water racing and adapted for basin terrain. Slim hull, aggressive sail geometry, and a prow designed for wake-disruption that can destabilize lighter rigs at close range. |
| **Crew** | 2-person: pilot-navigator, deck hand |
| **Starting CD** | 95 |
| **Strategy** | **Speed + Disruption.** Routes for moderate-tier nodes quickly, maintains mobility advantage, uses True Sight (Prism Falls boon) to identify hidden paths that larger teams can't follow. Loosely allied with Pudge's circle — they share tactical intel informally without a formal race alliance declaration. |
| **Weakness** | Not built for combat. If Marble Wall or Shatter Stamper engages them directly, they disengage. |
| **Notes** | Quietly sharp. Their east canyon path is found via Prism Falls — bypassing Marble Wall entirely. At the Dew Crystal standoff, they yield to Dancer & Fabian in what may or may not be a pre-arranged agreement. |

## Team 3: Marble Wall
| | |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Aggro/Hunter Team |
| **Rig** | **The Bastion** — A reinforced heavy crawler covered in reactive armor and forward-mounted kinetic dampeners. Less a racing vehicle and more a mobile blockade designed to absorb and dish out punishment. Does not have a traditional Harvest-Prong arm configuration — its prong is side-mounted and rarely deployed. |
| **Crew** | 4-person: driver, two weapon operators, navigator |
| **Starting CD** | 195 |
| **Strategy** | **Attrition & Elimination.** The Bastion's crew has no intention of winning by discharge. Their strategy is to destroy or disable faster, smaller rigs — starting with the highest-threat ultralight competitors — then occupy the central approach lane as an unclearable gate. The hope is to be the last moving rig by default. |
| **Weakness** | Never reaches CD ≤ 0. If every viable rig finds an alternative approach to the Resonator, Marble Wall's entire strategy collapses. |
| **Notes** | Disqualified post-race for continued aggression after the official call. They struck Dancer & Fabian's hull after Pudge discharged. |

## Team 4: Pudge
| | |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Experimental Light-Class |
| **Rig** | **The Gryphon-Hybrid** — A heavily customized, bulbous gyro-boat hull suspended beneath a racing gryphon via a three-point harness system. The hull looks aerodynamically absurd — round, low, built more for water than air — but the experimental Aero-Static components in the hull's undercarriage allow it to catch gryphon-generated thermals and extend flight time significantly. The umber gutter is oversized for the hull's rated class, because the gryphon's metabolic drive has to be artificially handicapped to keep the craft competitive. |
| **Crew** | 1 pilot + gryphon (organic drive) |
| **Starting CD** | 195 (classified Experimental; equal to Marble Wall on paper, for completely different reasons) |
| **Strategy** | **High-Risk Synchronization via Vertical Control.** Runs the north band exclusively, targeting Lift Stone + Umber Crystal for massive burst deficit reduction, then locks Loadlight active through Harmonic Lock long enough to ascend to altitude. The finish: aerial descent onto the Resonator apex. No team has ever done this. The rulebook doesn't prohibit it. |
| **Weakness** | Extremely vulnerable to early game attrition if the north band spires emerge late. If no major node spawns in the north, this strategy fails completely. |
| **Notes** | The pre-race "joke entry." Calculus Prime's 3.1% odds are technically generous. Professor Kante has been watching their training sessions for two seasons. |

## Team 5: Dancer & Fabian
| | |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Underdog / Precision Team |
| **Rig** | **The Glass-Sail** — A beautifully crafted, lightweight racing sailboat with one unique property: its hull material is capable of resonant-phasing, allowing it to compress through narrow gaps and deflect rather than absorb impacts. The lowest power rating in the competitive field. |
| **Crew** | 2-person: Dancer (helm/navigation), Fabian (prong/sail) |
| **Starting CD** | 55 |
| **Strategy** | **Agility, Evasion & Routing.** Routes for minor nodes in uncontested areas, plants Trail Mark boons for navigation memory, and uses the Glass-Sail's phasing capability as a key tactical asset — entering terrain other rigs cannot access. Their win condition is finding a path to the Resonator that Marble Wall cannot physically block. |
| **Weakness** | Any direct hit from a heavy rig ends the race for them. Zero armor buffer. |
| **Notes** | Standing ovation from the south bleachers at finish. Third place is an achievement given what they survived in the final 30 seconds. |

## Notable Soloists

### Stinger
| | |
|---|---|
| **Rig** | A minimalist, razor-thin hover-bike: little more than a seat, a single-crystal battery housing, and a forward-mounted Harvest-Prong the length of a jousting lance. |
| **Starting CD** | 45 — lowest in the field |
| **Strategy** | Extreme speed and tight-corridor weaving. Stinger's geometry lets it use canyon terrain as an evasion tool, threading gaps that no crewed rig can follow. One or two minor-to-moderate node syncs and they're at the Resonator. The margin for error against physical combat is zero. |
| **Notes** | **KO'd in Phase 3 by Marble Wall.** The Bastion tracked Stinger into the petrified forest with a kinetic dampener pulse that stuttered the hover-bike's lift mechanism mid-canyon. Crash webbing deployed; pilot recovered. |

### Goliath
| | |
|---|---|
| **Rig** | A massive land-ship, officially listed as a soloist but the size of a mobile fortress. Seven crew minimum. The hull alone is heavier than three Shatter Stampers combined. |
| **Starting CD** | 265 — highest in the field |
| **Strategy** | Survive "The Drop" and act as a moving terrain hazard. Goliath's strategic contribution to any race is to exist inside the basin and force route adaptation from everyone else. The crew does not realistically expect to discharge. |
| **Notes** | **DNF.** Still in the south canyon when the race is called. Technically still moving. The race marshals will not confirm or deny whether Goliath is still moving three hours later. |
