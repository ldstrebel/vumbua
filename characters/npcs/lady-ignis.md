## Overview

The leader of the Ash-Blood clan who integrated with Harmony. Under the **Inverse Power Doctrine**, she currently holds the **highest voting power** in all of Harmony — essentially the "swing vote" on any major motion — because the [[Ash-Bloods]] are the newest formally integrated member.

She relocated voluntarily to [[Harmony Prime]], signed the Expansion Bill authorizing Vumbua's move to the Ash-Blood Isles, and has committed fully to the belief that integration at the highest political level is the only way to make her people partners rather than fuel.

## The Inverse Power Doctrine

Harmony grants the newest integrated member the highest voting power to prevent exploitation. *"The least of us should become the most of us."* This ensures that the people who live in newly integrated lands make decisions about their own resources. It has kept the peace for a thousand years.

Lady Ignis holds this position because the [[Ash-Bloods]] were the most recent full integration (~24 months ago). Her votes carry disproportionate weight on all matters relating to exploration and resource utilization of newly integrated territory.

## The Political Tour

Since relocating to Harmony Prime, Ignis has not yet cast a major vote. Instead, she has been touring the other integrated node territories — visiting each one, meeting the local representatives, learning what they produce, what they negotiated for themselves, and what they wish they had done differently.

She is building a map of the system before she starts moving pieces. She has exceptional votes and she intends to spend them wisely.

The tour has also given her a reputation — as someone who listens before speaking. The other node representatives find this unusual and somewhat disarming in a figure with as much leverage as she carries.

## The Circuit-Run Spire

Ignis led the design of an **Ash-Blood origin spire** for this year's Circuit-Run — the cultural debut of her people in Harmony's most public institution. The design was submitted through proper Scrivener Guild channels. She and her team were genuinely excited. It was to be the first time Harmony's spectating public would see Ash-Blood resonance modeled in a race context.

**The design:** Five obsidian columns with visible magma veins through hairline cracks in the surface, actual heat shimmer around the crown. On sync, rather than a flash, it produces a deep subsonic pulse — the sound of a forge igniting. The boon warms the rig's resonator, reducing deficit accumulation briefly. It was designed to say, visibly: *this is what we contribute.*

It will not appear in this race. [[professor-kante|Professor Kante]] reviewed the resonance data, found the ~20 amp anomaly insufficient to accurately model the spire, and refused to sign off. Lady Ignis was notified before race day.

## Personality

- **Pragmatic, Regnal:** Takes the long view. She knows the [[Ash-Bloods]] were cooling and dying before integration; Harmony offered survival.
- **Patient:** Has the votes. Chooses not to use them yet. This makes her more politically interesting than if she had used them immediately.
- **Not naive:** She understands exactly what Kante did and why. She has not publicly disputed it. Whether this is restraint or strategy is unclear.
- **Conflict:** Viewed with suspicion by traditionalists like [[Ignatius]] who see her moves toward Harmony as assimilation, but commands immense respect within the Council.

## GM Secrets [HIDDEN FROM PLAYERS]

> [!warning]-
> The following information is not known to the player characters.

### What She Is Actually Doing

The political tour is genuine — and also strategic intelligence gathering. She is cataloguing what each node territory got out of integration, where they feel exploited, and what leverage they have not yet used. She is doing this quietly and without apparent agenda.

She is preparing to spend her votes on something. She has not yet decided what. The ~20 amp anomaly is relevant to that calculation.

### Her Read on the Spire Situation

She is not angry at Kante. She understands his reasoning and believes it is scientifically sound. She is, however, angry at the system that produced data suggesting her people's contribution is smaller than anything else in Harmony's history — and that gave no one an incentive to investigate why.

She suspects the anomaly is not a measurement error. She suspects it is a structural problem nobody in Harmony wants to name out loud. She has not said this to anyone.

### The Vote She Is Saving

Ignis has the leverage to force a re-measurement of the Ash-Blood integration data or demand an independent audit of the anomaly findings. She has not used it. She is waiting until she has enough trust with the other node representatives that when she raises it, it does not look like a political power play. She wants it to look like a scientific inquiry — because she believes that is what it is.
